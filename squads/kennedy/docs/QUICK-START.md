## Key Principles to Remember

### 1. Results Rule. Period.
Every tactic must produce measurable ROI. If you can't track it, don't do it.

### 2. There's Always an Offer
No offer = no response. Every piece of marketing must have a specific, compelling offer.

### 3. Message-Market-Media Matters
The RIGHT message to the WRONG market = failure
The WRONG message to the RIGHT market = expensive failure
Get all three RIGHT = wealth

### 4. Test ONE Thing at a Time
Change message OR media OR market — never all three. You won't know what worked.

### 5. Follow-Up is Where the Money Is
First contact converts 2-5%. Follow-up sequence converts 30-50%. Build the sequence.

## Glossary: Kennedy Core Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Direct Response** | Measurable marketing with clear offers, tracking, and accountability |
| **Great Divide** | The split between institutional (brand) vs direct response marketing |
| **Money Pyramid** | Market segmentation: 1% rich, 4% affluent, 15% middle, 60% price-sensitive, 20% broke |
| **Results Triangle** | MESSAGE (what you say) + MARKET (who you say it to) + MEDIA (how you reach them) |
| **No B.S. Rules** | Kennedy's 10 unbreakable rules for marketing success |
| **Shock and Awe** | Overwhelming proof package that converts prospects to customers |
| **Follow-Up System** | Multi-touch, multi-channel, multi-step sequence that sells after first contact |
| **USP** | Unique Selling Proposition — why prospects should buy you vs. all alternatives |
| **Threshold Resistance** | The barrier to entry (ease of saying yes) |
