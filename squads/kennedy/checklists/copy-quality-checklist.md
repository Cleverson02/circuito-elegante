# Copy Quality Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-copy
> Source: Copywriting Academy — Copy Quality Standards
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When reviewing any piece of sales copy for DR effectiveness

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Copy draft complete
- [ ] Target audience known
- [ ] Offer clearly defined

---

## STEP 1: Headline Assessment
- [ ] Headline promises a specific benefit or arouses curiosity
- [ ] Headline targets ONE specific reader
- [ ] Headline would survive in a sea of competing headlines
- [ ] Headline would make you stop scrolling or stop discarding mail
- [ ] Sub-headline supports and expands the main headline
- [ ] At least 20 headline alternatives were considered

## STEP 2: Opening Strength
- [ ] First paragraph is 1-3 sentences maximum
- [ ] Opening enters the conversation already in the prospect's mind
- [ ] No throat-clearing or preamble before the hook
- [ ] Opening earns the reader's attention for the next paragraph
- [ ] Pest-to-Welcome-Guest technique applied

## STEP 3: Body Copy Standards
- [ ] Written in conversational tone (not corporate)
- [ ] Short paragraphs throughout (2-4 lines)
- [ ] Subheadings break up text every 3-5 paragraphs
- [ ] Key phrases bolded, underlined, or highlighted
- [ ] "You" used more than "we" or "I"
- [ ] Specific numbers and details used (not vague claims)
- [ ] Stories included (personal, client, or illustrative)
- [ ] Transitions are smooth (each paragraph earns the next)

## STEP 4: Emotional Architecture
- [ ] Fear of loss is activated at some point
- [ ] Desire for gain is activated at some point
- [ ] Future pacing used (picture life AFTER purchase)
- [ ] Problem is agitated before solution is presented
- [ ] Emotional triggers layered throughout (not just one section)
- [ ] Reader can FEEL the transformation, not just understand it

## STEP 5: Proof and Credibility
- [ ] Testimonials present with full names (not "J.S. from New York")
- [ ] Case studies with specific, measurable results
- [ ] Credentials or authority markers established
- [ ] Third-party endorsements or media mentions
- [ ] Preponderance of proof (multiple proof types, not just one)

## STEP 6: Offer Presentation
- [ ] Offer clearly stated in a dedicated section
- [ ] Value anchoring (individual component values listed)
- [ ] Total value exceeds price by at least 5:1
- [ ] Bonuses listed with individual values
- [ ] Guarantee prominently displayed with specific terms
- [ ] Widget or premium included if applicable

## STEP 7: Call to Action
- [ ] CTA tells exactly what to do (specific instruction)
- [ ] CTA is repeated at least twice
- [ ] Multiple response channels provided
- [ ] Urgency tied to CTA (do this NOW)
- [ ] CTA could not be clearer or simpler

## STEP 8: P.S. Lines
- [ ] Minimum 2 P.S. lines included
- [ ] Each P.S. could sell the product on its own
- [ ] P.S. #1 restates main benefit and offer
- [ ] P.S. #2 adds urgency or additional benefit
- [ ] P.S. lines are not afterthoughts (they are strategic)

## STEP 9: Read Aloud Test
- [ ] Copy read aloud from start to finish
- [ ] Sounds like one person talking to another
- [ ] No awkward phrasing or corporate language
- [ ] Natural rhythm and flow throughout
- [ ] Any section that sounds "written" has been rewritten

## STEP 10: The "Would I Buy?" Test
- [ ] YOU would buy based on this copy
- [ ] A skeptical friend would find it compelling
- [ ] Every question the prospect might have is answered
- [ ] Nothing important is missing
- [ ] The copy is complete, not just finished

---

## Scoring
- 46-50/50 = A-grade copy — deploy with confidence
- 38-45/50 = Strong copy — minor refinements needed
- 28-37/50 = Needs work — several sections need strengthening
- Below 28 = Below standard — significant rewrite required
