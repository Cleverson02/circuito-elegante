# StorySelling Checklist — 4 Key Factors

> Agent: kennedy-brand
> Source: No B.S. Brand-Building by Direct Response — StorySelling System
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When creating brand narratives that sell

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Brand or personal story material available
- [ ] Target audience defined
- [ ] Key selling messages identified
- [ ] Willingness to be personal and vulnerable

---

## FACTOR 1: Connection

### 1.1 Origin Story
- [ ] How and why the business started is documented
- [ ] Story includes a personal struggle or challenge
- [ ] Story is relatable to the target audience
- [ ] Story creates empathy and identification

### 1.2 Shared Values
- [ ] Values communicated clearly through story
- [ ] Audience sees their own values reflected
- [ ] Story creates a sense of community or belonging
- [ ] Reader thinks "this person understands me"

### 1.3 Authenticity
- [ ] Story is genuine (not manufactured or corporate)
- [ ] Vulnerabilities and failures included (not just wins)
- [ ] Voice is personal and human
- [ ] Story could only be told by you (not generic)

---

## FACTOR 2: Fascination

### 2.1 Intrigue
- [ ] Something unexpected or unconventional in the story
- [ ] Curiosity hooks that make people want to learn more
- [ ] Elements that are conversation-worthy
- [ ] Story defies expectations

### 2.2 Drama
- [ ] Conflict or tension in the narrative
- [ ] Stakes are clear (what was at risk)
- [ ] Resolution is satisfying and instructive
- [ ] Emotional arc from problem to triumph

### 2.3 Uniqueness
- [ ] Story is genuinely different (not another "rags to riches")
- [ ] Specific details make it vivid and memorable
- [ ] Story has a hook that people remember and retell
- [ ] Brand story stands out from competitor stories

---

## FACTOR 3: Persuasion

### 3.1 Implicit Selling
- [ ] Story naturally leads to the value of what you offer
- [ ] Story demonstrates expertise without bragging
- [ ] Lessons from the story align with buying your product/service
- [ ] Reader is persuaded through narrative, not pitch

### 3.2 Trust Building
- [ ] Credibility established through story details
- [ ] Proof woven into narrative (results, endorsements, milestones)
- [ ] Character traits demonstrated through actions (not claimed)
- [ ] Reader trusts you MORE after hearing the story

### 3.3 Objection Handling
- [ ] Story addresses common objections implicitly
- [ ] Failures shown and overcome (reduces "too good to be true")
- [ ] Story shows the process, not just the result
- [ ] Reader's skepticism reduced through transparency

---

## FACTOR 4: Profit

### 4.1 Revenue Generation
- [ ] Story leads to a specific offer or call to action
- [ ] Story is deployed across marketing channels (not just told once)
- [ ] Story creates premium positioning (people pay more for a story)
- [ ] Story differentiates from commodity competitors

### 4.2 Repeat Use
- [ ] Story can be told in multiple formats (letter, video, speech, ad)
- [ ] Story elements can be referenced in ongoing marketing
- [ ] New chapters can be added as the business evolves
- [ ] Story becomes part of the brand DNA

### 4.3 Customer Loyalty
- [ ] Existing customers feel connected through the story
- [ ] Story gives customers a reason to stay beyond the product
- [ ] Customers retell your story to others (word of mouth)
- [ ] Story creates emotional switching costs

---

## Validation
- [ ] All 4 factors present in the brand story
- [ ] Story has been tested with target audience (resonance check)
- [ ] Story deployed across at least 3 marketing channels
- [ ] Story measurably contributes to sales (tracked)
- [ ] Story feels genuine and compelling (not forced)

---

## Scoring
- 28-32/32 = Powerful StorySelling — leverage across all marketing
- 22-27/32 = Strong story foundation — strengthen weak factor
- 16-21/32 = Story exists but not yet selling — integrate persuasion and profit
- Below 16 = No StorySelling in place — develop from Factor 1
