# Monthly DR Newsletter Design Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-media
> Source: Magnetic Marketing + No B.S. Direct Marketing — Newsletter Strategy
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When creating or auditing a monthly print newsletter

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Subscriber list established
- [ ] Content calendar planned
- [ ] Print vendor identified (if print format)
- [ ] Newsletter name and visual identity decided

---

## STEP 1: Newsletter Architecture (7 Mandatory Sections)

### 1.1 Personal Opening
- [ ] Conversational tone (like a letter from a friend)
- [ ] Personal anecdote or current observation
- [ ] Reader feels like they are getting insider access
- [ ] Sets the tone for the entire issue

### 1.2 Main Article
- [ ] Educational content with embedded selling
- [ ] Teaches a principle while referencing your products/services
- [ ] Substantial length (not a paragraph — a real article)
- [ ] Provides genuine value the reader can apply

### 1.3 Case Study / Testimonial
- [ ] Real client success story with specific results
- [ ] Before and after scenario described
- [ ] Reader can see themselves in the story
- [ ] Includes what the client did and the outcome

### 1.4 Special Offer
- [ ] Monthly promotion exclusive to subscribers
- [ ] Offer has a deadline (expire before next issue)
- [ ] Offer is compelling (not just a discount)
- [ ] Reward subscribers for being on the list

### 1.5 Q&A Section
- [ ] Real customer questions answered
- [ ] Questions showcase expertise
- [ ] Answers reference products/services naturally
- [ ] Invites readers to submit their own questions

### 1.6 Action Items
- [ ] Specific things to do this month
- [ ] Actionable and practical (not theoretical)
- [ ] Tied to the main article's teaching
- [ ] Creates forward momentum

### 1.7 P.S. / Back Page
- [ ] Additional offer or upcoming event promotion
- [ ] Tease next month's issue content
- [ ] Contact information prominent
- [ ] Call to action for some response

---

## STEP 2: Print vs Digital

### 2.1 Print (Strongly Preferred by Kennedy)
- [ ] Physical newsletter mailed to subscribers
- [ ] Quality paper stock used
- [ ] Stays on desks and gets passed around
- [ ] Cannot be deleted or ignored as easily as email
- [ ] Feels substantial and valuable

### 2.2 Digital (Supplementary Only)
- [ ] Digital version supplements (not replaces) print
- [ ] PDF version available for online subscribers if needed
- [ ] Email notification when print issue ships
- [ ] Digital content drives to print subscription

---

## STEP 3: Content Quality

### 3.1 Tone and Voice
- [ ] Personal and conversational (not corporate)
- [ ] Uses "you" and "I" language
- [ ] Opinionated and direct (no B.S.)
- [ ] Entertaining while being educational
- [ ] Reads like a personal communication, not a brochure

### 3.2 Value Density
- [ ] Every page has actionable content or compelling offers
- [ ] No filler content (every word earns its place)
- [ ] Reader can implement at least one idea from each issue
- [ ] Content is timeless enough to be referenced later

---

## STEP 4: Design Specs

### 4.1 Format
- [ ] Page count appropriate (4-12 pages typical)
- [ ] Consistent layout month to month
- [ ] Easy to read (font size, spacing, margins)
- [ ] Professional but not corporate (personality in design)

### 4.2 Visual Elements
- [ ] Photos with captions included
- [ ] Pull quotes or highlighted text
- [ ] Offer box clearly delineated
- [ ] Contact information on every page or spread

---

## Validation
- [ ] All 7 sections present in each issue
- [ ] At least 1 offer per issue (subscriber-exclusive)
- [ ] Content provides genuine value (would pay for it alone)
- [ ] Personal, conversational tone throughout
- [ ] Print format specified and production-ready

---

## Scoring
- 32-36/36 = Excellent newsletter — publish with confidence
- 26-31/36 = Good newsletter — strengthen weak sections
- 18-25/36 = Needs improvement — several sections missing or weak
- Below 18 = Not yet a newsletter — more of a brochure/email
