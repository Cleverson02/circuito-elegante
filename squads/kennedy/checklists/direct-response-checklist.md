# Direct Response Checklist — 10 No B.S. Rules Compliance

## Source
Dan Kennedy, "No B.S. Direct Marketing" (3rd Edition, 2018)

## Purpose
Validate any marketing piece, campaign, or strategy against Kennedy's
10 non-negotiable rules of direct marketing. Every piece of marketing
must pass ALL 10 rules before deployment.

---

## The 10 No B.S. Rules of Direct Marketing

### Rule #1: There Will Always Be an Offer
- [ ] The marketing piece contains a SPECIFIC offer (not just awareness)
- [ ] The offer states clearly WHAT the prospect gets
- [ ] The offer states clearly WHAT the prospect pays (or does)
- [ ] The offer includes BONUSES or premiums when appropriate
- [ ] The offer stands on its own (could be understood without the rest)

### Rule #2: There Will Be a Reason to Respond Right Now
- [ ] A deadline is stated (date, time, or event-based)
- [ ] The deadline is REAL (not perpetually extended)
- [ ] Scarcity is communicated (limited quantity, limited availability)
- [ ] A fast-action bonus exists for immediate responders
- [ ] Consequences of NOT acting are clearly stated

### Rule #3: There Will Be Clear Instructions
- [ ] The prospect knows EXACTLY how to respond
- [ ] Multiple response channels are provided (phone, web, mail, etc.)
- [ ] The response mechanism is EASY (not buried or confusing)
- [ ] Step-by-step instructions are included if process is complex
- [ ] A reply card, form, or order page is provided

### Rule #4: There Will Be Tracking and Measurement
- [ ] Unique tracking code or source identifier on the piece
- [ ] Unique phone number for this campaign (or extension)
- [ ] Unique URL or landing page for this campaign
- [ ] ROI calculation method defined BEFORE launch
- [ ] Response rate targets established
- [ ] Cost per lead / cost per acquisition calculated

### Rule #5: There Will Be Meaningful Follow-Up
- [ ] Follow-up sequence designed (minimum 5 steps)
- [ ] Follow-up is multi-media (not just email)
- [ ] Follow-up timeline mapped (Day 1, 3, 7, 14, 21, etc.)
- [ ] Non-responder sequence defined
- [ ] Follow-up includes new angles/approaches (not just reminders)

### Rule #6: Results Rule — No Vanity Metrics
- [ ] Success measured by REVENUE, not impressions/likes/clicks
- [ ] ROI is the primary success metric
- [ ] Lifetime customer value considered
- [ ] Results compared to cost (not just gross numbers)
- [ ] A/B testing planned to optimize results

### Rule #7: There Will Be Tough Love
- [ ] The marketing tells the TRUTH (even uncomfortable truth)
- [ ] The marketing pre-qualifies (repels bad-fit prospects)
- [ ] The marketing uses specifics, not vague promises
- [ ] Claims are backed by proof (testimonials, data, case studies)
- [ ] No institutional/branding fluff hiding the truth

### Rule #8: There Will Be Thick Skin
- [ ] The marketing is bold enough to stand out
- [ ] Willingness to polarize (not trying to please everyone)
- [ ] Long copy is used when appropriate (not shortened for fear)
- [ ] Direct, assertive language is used (not passive/meek)
- [ ] The marketing says what competitors won't say

### Rule #9: Be in the Market (Not Above It)
- [ ] The marketing uses language the PROSPECT uses (not jargon)
- [ ] The marketing enters the conversation in the prospect's mind
- [ ] The marketing addresses real problems (not assumed ones)
- [ ] The marketing was created with actual market research
- [ ] The marketing is tested with real prospects before scaling

### Rule #10: Willing to Be "Bad" (Direct Response Over Pretty)
- [ ] Function over form (conversion over aesthetics)
- [ ] Long copy used when needed (not trimmed for design)
- [ ] Direct calls to action (not subtle or clever)
- [ ] Urgency/scarcity applied even if it "looks salesy"
- [ ] Results prioritized over design awards

## Scoring

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 10/10 | Excellent | Deploy with confidence |
| 8-9/10 | Good | Deploy, fix remaining items within 7 days |
| 6-7/10 | Needs Work | Fix before deploying |
| 4-5/10 | Poor | Significant rewrite needed |
| 0-3/10 | Failing | Start over with DR principles |

**Kennedy says:** "A score below 8 means you're wasting money. Fix it or kill it."

---

## Quick Pass/Fail

If ANY of these are missing, the piece FAILS regardless of other scores:
1. [ ] Has a specific offer (Rule #1)
2. [ ] Has tracking/measurement (Rule #4)
3. [ ] Has a call to action (Rule #3)

These three are the "iron triangle" of direct response. Without all three,
it's not direct response marketing — it's institutional advertising.
