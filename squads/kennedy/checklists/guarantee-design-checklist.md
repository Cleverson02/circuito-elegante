# Guarantee Design Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-offers
> Source: The Ultimate Sales Letter + Ultimate Marketing Plan — Risk Reversal
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When designing a guarantee that eliminates buyer risk

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Product/service delivery capability confirmed
- [ ] Refund/return process defined
- [ ] Legal review of guarantee terms planned
- [ ] Confidence in product/service quality established

---

## STEP 1: Guarantee Type Selection

### 1.1 Standard Risk Reversal
- [ ] Money-back guarantee with clear duration (30/60/90 days)
- [ ] No questions asked policy (truly no questions)
- [ ] Process is simple (not bureaucratic)
- [ ] Refund issued promptly (within X business days)

### 1.2 Better-Than-Risk-Reversal
- [ ] Customer keeps bonuses even if they request a refund
- [ ] Additional compensation for their time if not satisfied
- [ ] "Keep everything plus your money back" offer
- [ ] Creates genuine risk on the SELLER (not just removing buyer risk)

### 1.3 Performance Guarantee
- [ ] Specific result guaranteed (not vague "satisfaction")
- [ ] Clear measurement criteria defined
- [ ] Timeline for achieving results stated
- [ ] What happens if result is not achieved clearly documented

### 1.4 Conditional Guarantee
- [ ] Customer must complete specific steps to qualify
- [ ] Steps are reasonable and achievable
- [ ] Conditions create engagement (increasing chance of success)
- [ ] Both parties have obligations clearly stated

---

## STEP 2: Guarantee Construction

### 2.1 Specificity
- [ ] Duration stated precisely (not "anytime")
- [ ] Process spelled out step by step
- [ ] Contact method provided for refund requests
- [ ] Conditions (if any) are crystal clear

### 2.2 Boldness
- [ ] Guarantee is the strongest you can ethically offer
- [ ] Kennedy test: "If you truly believe in your product, prove it"
- [ ] Guarantee creates a "they'd be crazy not to try" reaction
- [ ] Guarantee addresses the #1 buying objection

### 2.3 Presentation
- [ ] Guarantee has its own dedicated section (not buried)
- [ ] Certificate-style box or visual treatment
- [ ] Personal signature or seal of guarantee
- [ ] Language is confident and assertive (not apologetic)
- [ ] Guarantee is referenced in P.S. lines

---

## STEP 3: Guarantee Integration

### 3.1 Copy Integration
- [ ] Guarantee introduced at the right moment (after value is established)
- [ ] Guarantee reframes the decision as risk-free
- [ ] Guarantee language reinforces product confidence
- [ ] Guarantee used as a selling tool (not just a legal obligation)

### 3.2 Objection Handling
- [ ] "What if it doesn't work for me?" answered by the guarantee
- [ ] "What if I change my mind?" answered by the guarantee
- [ ] "Is this a scam?" reduced by the guarantee
- [ ] "Can I afford to risk this?" eliminated by the guarantee

---

## STEP 4: Operational Preparation

### 4.1 Fulfillment
- [ ] Refund process documented and staff trained
- [ ] Refund approval workflow defined
- [ ] Refund tracking system in place
- [ ] Expected refund rate projected (budget for it)

### 4.2 Learning
- [ ] Refund reasons collected (improve product)
- [ ] Refund rate monitored monthly
- [ ] If rate exceeds projection, product is improved (not guarantee weakened)
- [ ] Exit surveys used to understand refund drivers

---

## Validation
- [ ] Guarantee type appropriate for the product/price point
- [ ] Guarantee is bolder than any competitor's
- [ ] Operational readiness confirmed (can fulfill refund requests)
- [ ] Legal review completed
- [ ] Guarantee presented prominently in all sales materials

---

## Scoring
- 26-30/30 = Rock-solid guarantee — deploy with confidence
- 20-25/30 = Good guarantee — strengthen presentation or boldness
- 14-19/30 = Adequate but not compelling — prospects still feel risk
- Below 14 = Guarantee is weak or absent — redesign needed
