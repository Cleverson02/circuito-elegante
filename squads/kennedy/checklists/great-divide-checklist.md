# The Great Divide Analysis Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-direct-response
> Source: No B.S. Direct Marketing — The Great Divide (DR vs Institutional)
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When auditing marketing to classify as Direct Response vs Institutional

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] All current marketing materials gathered
- [ ] Marketing budget itemized by channel/piece
- [ ] Current results data available (if any)

---

## THE GREAT DIVIDE

Kennedy's fundamental distinction: ALL marketing falls on one side of The Great Divide.

| DR Marketing | Institutional Marketing |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Measurable | Immeasurable |
| Accountable | Unaccountable |
| Asks for response | Builds awareness |
| Tracks ROI | Tracks impressions |
| Sells NOW | Builds brand over time |
| Direct to customer | Through intermediaries |

---

## STEP 1: Classification of Current Marketing

For each marketing piece or campaign, check which side of the divide:

### 1.1 Direct Response Indicators
- [ ] Has a specific offer (not just "visit our website")
- [ ] Has a deadline or urgency mechanism
- [ ] Has clear call to action (specific next step)
- [ ] Has tracking mechanism (unique code, phone, URL)
- [ ] Has measurable results (can calculate ROI)
- [ ] Asks the prospect to DO something NOW

### 1.2 Institutional Indicators
- [ ] No specific offer (just brand awareness)
- [ ] No deadline or urgency
- [ ] No clear call to action
- [ ] No tracking mechanism
- [ ] Results are "brand awareness" or "impressions"
- [ ] Goal is to be "remembered" or "recognized"

---

## STEP 2: Budget Audit

### 2.1 Current Allocation
- [ ] Total marketing budget documented
- [ ] Each line item classified as DR or Institutional
- [ ] Percentage of budget in DR vs Institutional calculated
- [ ] ROI documented for DR pieces (if available)

### 2.2 Kennedy's Standard
- [ ] Target: 80%+ of budget should be DR
- [ ] Current DR percentage: ____%
- [ ] Current Institutional percentage: ____%
- [ ] Gap from 80/20 target identified

---

## STEP 3: Conversion Plan

### 3.1 Convert Institutional to DR
For each institutional piece, can it be converted?
- [ ] Add a specific offer
- [ ] Add a deadline or urgency
- [ ] Add a clear call to action
- [ ] Add a tracking mechanism
- [ ] If all four can be added: CONVERT
- [ ] If none can be added: ELIMINATE

### 3.2 Elimination Plan
- [ ] Identify marketing spend that cannot be made DR
- [ ] Calculate annual savings from elimination
- [ ] Plan reallocation to DR channels
- [ ] Timeline for phase-out established

---

## STEP 4: Ongoing Enforcement

### 4.1 New Marketing Review
- [ ] Every new marketing piece checked against Great Divide
- [ ] DR checklist applied before any marketing spend approved
- [ ] No institutional advertising without DR component
- [ ] Team educated on the distinction

### 4.2 Monthly Review
- [ ] Monthly marketing spend classified
- [ ] DR vs Institutional ratio maintained at 80/20+
- [ ] Underperforming DR pieces identified and improved
- [ ] Any institutional creep identified and corrected

---

## Validation
- [ ] All current marketing classified as DR or Institutional
- [ ] Budget reallocation plan created
- [ ] DR percentage trending toward 80%+
- [ ] No new institutional-only marketing approved

---

## Scoring
- 22-26/26 = True DR marketer — on the right side of the divide
- 18-21/26 = Mostly DR — institutional elements creeping in
- 12-17/26 = Mixed — significant institutional spend needs conversion
- Below 12 = Predominantly institutional — major shift needed
