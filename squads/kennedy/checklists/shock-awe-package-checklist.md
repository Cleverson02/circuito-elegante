# Shock and Awe Package Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-magnetic
> Source: Magnetic Marketing — Shock and Awe Package System
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When creating a conversion package that overwhelms prospects with value and proof

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Lead magnet and lead generation system in place
- [ ] Target market and their key objections identified
- [ ] Testimonials and case studies collected
- [ ] Budget for physical package production allocated

---

## STEP 1: Package Contents

### 1.1 Core Information
- [ ] Detailed information about your product/service/solution
- [ ] FAQ document addressing top 10 questions
- [ ] Comparison chart (you vs. alternatives)
- [ ] Process overview (what happens when they buy)

### 1.2 Proof Elements
- [ ] Minimum 5 testimonials with full names and photos
- [ ] At least 2 detailed case studies with measurable results
- [ ] Media mentions, press coverage, or publications
- [ ] Awards, certifications, or industry recognition
- [ ] Client list or "as seen in" logos

### 1.3 Credibility Builders
- [ ] Personal bio/story of the founder/expert
- [ ] Professional credentials and qualifications
- [ ] Published articles or book excerpts
- [ ] Speaking engagements or expert appearances

### 1.4 Value-Add Items
- [ ] Free report or guide (related to their problem)
- [ ] Checklist, worksheet, or planning tool
- [ ] Sample or demonstration of the product
- [ ] DVD, USB drive, or access card for video content

---

## STEP 2: Package Design

### 2.1 Physical Presentation
- [ ] Arrives in a substantial package (not a thin envelope)
- [ ] Professional printing and materials used
- [ ] Branded folder, binder, or box
- [ ] Heavy enough to feel important

### 2.2 Impact
- [ ] Opening the package creates a "wow" moment
- [ ] Quantity of materials is impressive (thick, substantial)
- [ ] Organization makes it easy to navigate
- [ ] Personal touch included (handwritten note, personal letter)

### 2.3 Flow
- [ ] Clear "start here" instruction included
- [ ] Contents listed with suggested reading order
- [ ] Cover letter guides recipient through the package
- [ ] Final piece is a strong call to action

---

## STEP 3: Delivery Timing

### 3.1 Speed
- [ ] Sent within 24 hours of lead capture (ideally same day)
- [ ] Shipping method ensures arrival within 2-3 business days
- [ ] Pre-arrival email or text alerting them to watch for the package
- [ ] Follow-up call within 24-48 hours of delivery

### 3.2 Follow-Up Integration
- [ ] Day 1: Package ships + confirmation email
- [ ] Day 3-4: "Did you receive the package?" call/email
- [ ] Day 7: Follow-up with specific reference to package contents
- [ ] Day 14: Final follow-up before closing the window
- [ ] Day 30: Long-term nurture if not converted

---

## Validation
- [ ] Package has minimum 5 distinct pieces of content
- [ ] At least 10 proof elements included across all materials
- [ ] Package cannot be easily replicated by competitors
- [ ] Recipient would feel guilty throwing it away (too valuable)
- [ ] Package creates a "pile of proof" effect on the desk

---

## Scoring
- 26-30/30 = Outstanding Shock and Awe package — deploy
- 20-25/30 = Strong package — add missing elements
- 14-19/30 = Needs more substance — too thin to overwhelm
- Below 14 = Not yet a Shock and Awe — more of a brochure
