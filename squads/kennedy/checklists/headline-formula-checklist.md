# Headline Formula Checklist — 12 Fill-in-the-Blank Systems

> Agent: kennedy-sales-letter
> Source: The Ultimate Sales Letter — 12 Fill-in-the-Blank Headline Formulas
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When creating headlines for sales letters, ads, emails, or any marketing piece

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Target market clearly defined
- [ ] Primary benefit identified
- [ ] USP articulated
- [ ] Minimum 20 headline candidates required before selecting one

---

## THE 12 HEADLINE FORMULAS

### Formula 1: "They Didn't Think I Could _____, But I Did"
- [ ] Personal achievement story angle tested
- [ ] Underdog or skeptic-overcoming angle present
- [ ] Creates curiosity about HOW
- Example: "They Laughed When I Sat Down at the Piano — But When I Started to Play..."

### Formula 2: "Who Else Wants _____?"
- [ ] Social proof implied (others already want this)
- [ ] Invites reader to join a desirable group
- [ ] Benefit clearly stated
- Example: "Who Else Wants a Guaranteed Way to Get More Referrals?"

### Formula 3: "How To _____ [Desirable Outcome]"
- [ ] Simple, direct promise of knowledge
- [ ] Outcome is specific and desirable
- [ ] Reader knows exactly what they will learn
- Example: "How to Get Your Dentist Patients to Refer 3 New Patients Per Month"

### Formula 4: "Are You _____?"
- [ ] Question targets a specific frustration or desire
- [ ] Reader self-selects (yes or no)
- [ ] Creates immediate engagement
- Example: "Are You Making These 9 Fatal Marketing Mistakes?"

### Formula 5: "How I _____ [Impressive Result]"
- [ ] First-person success story
- [ ] Specific, impressive result cited
- [ ] Creates curiosity about the method
- Example: "How I Turned a $500 Ad Into $47,000 in Sales in 30 Days"

### Formula 6: "If You _____, You Can _____"
- [ ] Conditional statement that qualifies the reader
- [ ] Promise is tied to a reasonable condition
- [ ] Makes the reader feel in control
- Example: "If You Can Spare 20 Minutes a Day, You Can Build a 6-Figure Side Income"

### Formula 7: "_____ Secrets of _____"
- [ ] Insider knowledge promised
- [ ] Specific number adds credibility
- [ ] Aspirational source cited
- Example: "7 Secrets of Millionaire Entrepreneurs That Wall Street Doesn't Want You to Know"

### Formula 8: "Warning: _____"
- [ ] Fear-based attention grabber
- [ ] Warns against a real danger or mistake
- [ ] Creates urgency to read more
- Example: "Warning: 3 Things Your Financial Advisor Will Never Tell You"

### Formula 9: "Give Me _____ and I'll _____"
- [ ] Clear exchange proposed (time/effort for result)
- [ ] Reader knows exactly what is required
- [ ] Promise is specific and compelling
- Example: "Give Me 5 Days and I'll Give You a Magnetic Personality"

### Formula 10: "_____ Ways to _____"
- [ ] Numbered list promises multiple solutions
- [ ] Curiosity about which ways
- [ ] Odd numbers work better than even
- Example: "101 Ways to Increase Your Direct Mail Response Rate"

### Formula 11: "The _____ Method/System/Secret"
- [ ] Named, proprietary approach
- [ ] Implies proven, systematic solution
- [ ] Creates brand around the method
- Example: "The Magnetic Marketing System That Generates Leads on Autopilot"

### Formula 12: "Do You Make These _____?"
- [ ] Fear of making mistakes
- [ ] Self-assessment curiosity
- [ ] Reader wants to check themselves
- Example: "Do You Make These 9 Fatal Marketing Mistakes?"

## HEADLINE QUALITY CHECK

### Must-Have Elements
- [ ] Headline targets ONE specific reader (not masses)
- [ ] Headline promises a BENEFIT or arouses CURIOSITY (or both)
- [ ] Headline passes the A-Pile test (would you stop and read?)
- [ ] Headline is specific (numbers, timeframes, results)
- [ ] Headline could stand alone as a mini-ad

### Must-Avoid Elements
- [ ] NO clever wordplay that obscures the benefit
- [ ] NO industry jargon the prospect would not understand
- [ ] NO vague promises ("the best," "amazing results")
- [ ] NO headline that requires context to understand
- [ ] NO headline that is about YOU instead of the READER

---

## Selection Process
- [ ] Minimum 20 headline candidates written
- [ ] Top 5 selected for further development
- [ ] Sub-headlines written for top 5
- [ ] Final headline selected based on A-Pile test
- [ ] Split test plan created for top 2 candidates

---

## Scoring
- 18-20/20 quality checks = Headline is ready to deploy
- 14-17/20 = Good headline, refine weak areas
- 10-13/20 = Average headline, significant improvement needed
- Below 10 = Headline is weak, start over with formulas
