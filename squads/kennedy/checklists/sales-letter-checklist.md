# Sales Letter Checklist — 28-Step System Validation

## Source
Dan Kennedy, "The Ultimate Sales Letter" (3rd Edition, 2006)

## Purpose
Validate a sales letter against Kennedy's complete 28-Step System.
Every sales letter must follow this systematic process. Skipping steps
produces weaker letters. The system works because it is a SYSTEM.

---

## SECTION 1: PREPARATION (Steps 1-6)

### Step 1: Get "Ready"
- [ ] All research materials gathered
- [ ] Competitor letters collected and analyzed
- [ ] Product/service documentation reviewed
- [ ] Testimonials and proof elements collected

### Step 2: Get into the Prospect's Shoes (Demographics)
- [ ] Age, gender, income level identified
- [ ] Geographic location considered
- [ ] Occupation and industry mapped
- [ ] Education level understood

### Step 3: Get into the Prospect's Shoes (Psychographics)
- [ ] Fears and anxieties identified
- [ ] Frustrations and complaints catalogued
- [ ] Desires and aspirations mapped
- [ ] Current solutions they have tried listed
- [ ] What keeps them awake at 3 AM documented

### Step 4: Get into the Prospect's Shoes (Buying Behavior)
- [ ] How they typically buy (impulse, research, referral)
- [ ] Price sensitivity level assessed
- [ ] Decision-making process understood (solo, committee, spouse)
- [ ] Past buying patterns for similar products reviewed

### Step 5: Prioritize the Benefits
- [ ] All features listed and converted to benefits
- [ ] Benefits ranked by importance to the prospect
- [ ] Top 3 benefits identified as primary drivers
- [ ] "So what?" test applied to each benefit
- [ ] Benefits expressed in prospect's language

### Step 6: Build the "Damaging Admission"
- [ ] Honest admission of limitation or weakness included
- [ ] Admission builds credibility (not destroys it)
- [ ] Admission is followed by a "but" that strengthens the case
- [ ] Admission differentiates from competitors who claim perfection

---

## SECTION 2: HEADLINE + OPENING (Steps 7-10)

### Step 7: Create the Headline
- [ ] Minimum 20 headline candidates written
- [ ] Kennedy's 12 Fill-in-the-Blank Formulas used
- [ ] Headline promises a BENEFIT or arouses CURIOSITY
- [ ] Headline passes the "A-Pile" test (would you open this?)
- [ ] Headline speaks to ONE specific reader (not masses)
- [ ] Sub-headline supports and expands the main headline

### Step 8: Develop the Opening
- [ ] Opens with the prospect's problem (not your product)
- [ ] Enters the conversation already in the prospect's mind
- [ ] Creates immediate rapport and identification
- [ ] Uses Pest-to-Welcome-Guest technique
- [ ] First paragraph earns the second paragraph

### Step 9: Create the First Paragraph "Speed Bump"
- [ ] First paragraph is short (1-3 sentences maximum)
- [ ] First paragraph hooks the reader to continue
- [ ] No preamble or throat-clearing before the hook
- [ ] Reader immediately sees relevance to THEIR life

### Step 10: Build the Story
- [ ] Personal or client story included
- [ ] Story demonstrates the transformation
- [ ] Story creates emotional connection
- [ ] Story is specific (names, dates, numbers when possible)
- [ ] Story bridges to the solution naturally

---

## SECTION 3: BODY COPY (Steps 11-18)

### Step 11: Present Your Credentials
- [ ] Relevant credentials stated (not bragging, proving)
- [ ] Third-party validation included (media, awards, associations)
- [ ] "Why listen to me?" question answered
- [ ] Credentials are relevant to the prospect's problem

### Step 12: Show Proof
- [ ] Testimonials included (specific, not generic)
- [ ] Case studies with measurable results
- [ ] Before/after comparisons
- [ ] Expert endorsements if available
- [ ] Statistics and data points
- [ ] "Preponderance of proof" achieved (multiple proof types)

### Step 13: Create Fascination Bullets
- [ ] Minimum 30 fascination bullets written
- [ ] Mix of bullet types used (blind, revelation, contrarian, etc.)
- [ ] Each bullet creates curiosity without satisfying it
- [ ] Bullets highlight unique and surprising benefits
- [ ] Bullets are specific (numbers, names, details)

### Step 14: Build the Case (Problem-Agitate-Solve)
- [ ] Problem clearly stated and validated
- [ ] Problem AGITATED (made more urgent, more painful)
- [ ] Solution presented as the logical answer
- [ ] Solution is positioned as unique and superior
- [ ] Logical argument supports emotional decision

### Step 15: Overcome Objections
- [ ] Top 5 objections identified and addressed
- [ ] Objections handled pre-emptively (before reader thinks them)
- [ ] FAQ section included if appropriate
- [ ] "Damaging admission" used strategically
- [ ] Price objection specifically addressed

### Step 16: Build Perceived Value
- [ ] Value of each component stated individually
- [ ] Total value far exceeds the asking price
- [ ] Value comparison to alternatives provided
- [ ] "If you just use ONE idea..." value statement included
- [ ] ROI calculation or payback period shown

### Step 17: Invoke Emotion
- [ ] Emotional triggers layered throughout (not just one section)
- [ ] Fear of loss + desire for gain both used
- [ ] Future pacing (paint picture of life AFTER purchase)
- [ ] Social proof creates "bandwagon" effect
- [ ] Reader can FEEL the transformation, not just understand it

### Step 18: Create Urgency
- [ ] Genuine deadline stated
- [ ] Scarcity mechanism in place
- [ ] Fast-action bonus offered
- [ ] Consequences of delay spelled out
- [ ] Urgency is REAL (not manufactured)

---

## SECTION 4: THE CLOSE (Steps 19-24)

### Step 19: Present the Offer
- [ ] Offer clearly stated in one dedicated section
- [ ] Everything included in the offer listed
- [ ] Price stated clearly (no hidden fees)
- [ ] Payment options available if applicable
- [ ] Offer is IRRESISTIBLE (hard to say no)

### Step 20: Add the Guarantee
- [ ] Guarantee removes risk from the prospect
- [ ] Guarantee is specific (duration, conditions, process)
- [ ] Guarantee is prominently displayed (not buried)
- [ ] "Better than risk reversal" considered
- [ ] Guarantee language is confident, not apologetic

### Step 21: Add Bonuses
- [ ] Minimum 3 bonuses included
- [ ] Each bonus has its own stated value
- [ ] Bonuses solve adjacent problems (not just more of the same)
- [ ] At least one bonus is time-sensitive
- [ ] Bonuses feel like genuine added value (not filler)

### Step 22: Write the Call to Action
- [ ] CTA is CLEAR and SPECIFIC
- [ ] CTA tells exactly what to do next
- [ ] CTA is repeated at least twice
- [ ] Multiple response channels offered
- [ ] CTA creates urgency ("do this NOW, not later")

### Step 23: Add Premium/Widget
- [ ] Tangible premium or widget included (if applicable)
- [ ] Premium has perceived high value
- [ ] Premium is "keep regardless" (even if they return main offer)
- [ ] Premium ties thematically to the offer

### Step 24: Write the P.S. Lines
- [ ] Minimum 2 P.S. lines included
- [ ] P.S. #1 restates the main benefit and offer
- [ ] P.S. #2 adds urgency or additional benefit
- [ ] P.S. #3 includes testimonial or proof (optional)
- [ ] Each P.S. could sell the product on its own
- [ ] P.S. treated as a "mini sales letter" (many read P.S. first)

---

## SECTION 5: FINAL REVIEW (Steps 25-28)

### Step 25: Read Aloud Test
- [ ] Entire letter read aloud
- [ ] Sounds like one person talking to another
- [ ] No corporate-speak or jargon
- [ ] Natural rhythm and flow
- [ ] Any awkward sections rewritten

### Step 26: Graphic Enhancement
- [ ] Key phrases bolded, underlined, or highlighted
- [ ] Subheadings break up long text blocks
- [ ] Testimonials in boxes or callouts
- [ ] Guarantee in a certificate-style box
- [ ] Easy to scan and skim (readers scan before reading)

### Step 27: Technical Review
- [ ] No spelling or grammar errors
- [ ] All links and URLs work
- [ ] Phone numbers are correct
- [ ] Pricing is consistent throughout
- [ ] Legal compliance checked (disclaimers, FTC, etc.)

### Step 28: Final "Would I Buy?" Test
- [ ] YOU would buy based on this letter
- [ ] A skeptical friend would find it compelling
- [ ] The letter answers every question the prospect might have
- [ ] Nothing important is missing
- [ ] The letter is complete, not just finished
