# Fascination Bullets Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-copy
> Source: Copywriting Academy + Fascination Marketing System — Bullet Writing System
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When creating fascination bullets for sales letters, ads, or marketing pieces

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Product/service thoroughly understood (all features and benefits)
- [ ] Target prospect's desires, fears, and curiosities mapped
- [ ] Minimum 30 bullets required per sales letter
- [ ] Source material available (product details, results, stories)

---

## STEP 1: Bullet Types — Create at Least 3 of Each

### 1.1 Blind Fascination
- [ ] Teases a secret or method without revealing it
- [ ] Creates unbearable curiosity
- [ ] Reader MUST buy to find out
- [ ] Example: "The 3-word phrase that instantly disarms even the most hostile prospect (page 47)"

### 1.2 Revelation Bullet
- [ ] Promises to reveal something hidden or unknown
- [ ] Uses "discover," "revealed," "exposed" language
- [ ] Information feels exclusive or insider
- [ ] Example: "The little-known tax loophole that saves the average business owner $37,400 a year"

### 1.3 Contrarian Bullet
- [ ] Challenges conventional wisdom
- [ ] Makes reader question what they "know"
- [ ] Creates cognitive dissonance (must resolve by reading)
- [ ] Example: "Why your best customers are actually HURTING your business (and what to do instead)"

### 1.4 Specificity Bullet
- [ ] Packed with specific numbers, names, and details
- [ ] Credibility through precision
- [ ] More specific = more believable
- [ ] Example: "How a 43-year-old plumber from Des Moines added $147,832 to his annual revenue in 90 days"

### 1.5 Curiosity Bullet
- [ ] Opens a loop that MUST be closed
- [ ] Reader cannot guess the answer
- [ ] Information gap creates desire
- [ ] Example: "The #1 mistake 94% of business owners make with their website (hint: it has nothing to do with design)"

### 1.6 Fear Bullet
- [ ] Warns of a danger or loss
- [ ] Creates urgency to learn the solution
- [ ] Taps into existing anxieties
- [ ] Example: "Warning: This common email practice could get your entire account blacklisted overnight"

### 1.7 How-To Bullet
- [ ] Promises practical, actionable knowledge
- [ ] Specific outcome or result stated
- [ ] Reader sees immediate applicability
- [ ] Example: "How to double your referrals without asking a single person (using the 'Champion Circle' method)"

### 1.8 Secret Bullet
- [ ] Promises access to privileged information
- [ ] Insider language used (methods the elite use)
- [ ] Creates exclusivity and desire
- [ ] Example: "The negotiation secret that Fortune 500 CEOs use but will never admit publicly"

### 1.9 Warning Bullet
- [ ] Warns against a mistake, danger, or trap
- [ ] Creates protective urgency (must know to avoid harm)
- [ ] Positions the offer as essential protection
- [ ] Example: "3 things your financial advisor hopes you never find out about mutual fund fees"

---

## STEP 2: Quality Standards for Each Bullet

### 2.1 Curiosity Gap
- [ ] Each bullet creates a gap between what reader knows and wants to know
- [ ] Gap is specific enough to create desire but vague enough to not satisfy
- [ ] Reader cannot "figure out" the answer without buying
- [ ] Bullet makes reader say "I NEED to know that"

### 2.2 Benefit Implied
- [ ] Each bullet implies a benefit to the reader
- [ ] Bullet is about THEM, not about the product
- [ ] Reader sees how this knowledge helps their situation
- [ ] Even fear bullets imply protection (a benefit)

### 2.3 Specificity
- [ ] Numbers used where possible (exact figures, not "many")
- [ ] Names, dates, or locations included where relevant
- [ ] Specific outcomes stated (not vague promises)
- [ ] Precision signals truthfulness

---

## STEP 3: Bullet Set Review

### 3.1 Variety
- [ ] At least 5 different bullet types used in the set
- [ ] No more than 3 consecutive bullets of the same type
- [ ] Mix of positive (desire) and negative (fear) bullets
- [ ] Some bullets are 1 line, some are 2-3 lines (rhythm variety)

### 3.2 Power
- [ ] The strongest 5 bullets identified and placed strategically
- [ ] First bullet is a showstopper (best curiosity gap)
- [ ] Last bullet is a kicker (leaves them wanting more)
- [ ] Weakest bullets removed (quality over quantity past minimum 30)

### 3.3 Integration
- [ ] Bullets placed at strategic points in the letter (not just one section)
- [ ] Bullets support the main argument of the copy
- [ ] Bullets create desire for the specific product being sold
- [ ] Bullets do NOT accidentally reveal the answer (defeating the purpose)

---

## Validation
- [ ] Minimum 30 bullets written
- [ ] At least 5 bullet types represented
- [ ] Each bullet passes the "would I want to know?" test
- [ ] No bullet accidentally gives away the answer
- [ ] Bullets ranked by power (top 10 identified)

---

## Scoring
- 28-32/32 = Bullet set is irresistible — deploy
- 22-27/32 = Strong bullets — refine the weaker ones
- 16-21/32 = Needs more variety and power
- Below 16 = Bullet set is weak — rewrite with formulas
