# Time Vampires Elimination Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-advisor
> Source: Renegade Millionaire + No B.S. Time Management — Time Vampires Framework
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When auditing time management and eliminating productivity killers

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] 1-week time log completed (how you actually spend time)
- [ ] Current hourly value calculated (annual income / productive hours)
- [ ] Calendar available for review
- [ ] Willingness to make uncomfortable changes

---

## STEP 1: Identify Time Vampires

### 1.1 People Vampires
- [ ] Identify who consumes your time without producing value
- [ ] Drop-in visitors catalogued (who, how often, how long)
- [ ] Unnecessary meeting participants identified
- [ ] Energy-draining relationships recognized
- [ ] Clients who consume disproportionate time vs. revenue identified

### 1.2 Communication Vampires
- [ ] Unscheduled phone calls tracked (who, frequency, duration)
- [ ] Email checking frequency analyzed (how often per day)
- [ ] Text/message interruptions counted
- [ ] Social media time audited
- [ ] "Quick question" interruptions logged

### 1.3 System Vampires
- [ ] Meetings that could be emails identified
- [ ] Redundant processes found
- [ ] Manual tasks that could be automated
- [ ] Inefficient technology or tools
- [ ] Travel time that could be reduced

### 1.4 Self-Vampires
- [ ] Procrastination patterns identified
- [ ] Perfectionism that delays completion
- [ ] Tasks below your pay grade that you still do
- [ ] "Busy work" that feels productive but isn't
- [ ] Decision-making delays

---

## STEP 2: Eliminate and Protect

### 2.1 Boundary Setting
- [ ] Designated "no interruption" blocks on calendar
- [ ] Phone hours established (not always available)
- [ ] Email checking limited to 2-3 times per day
- [ ] Meeting policy established (agenda required, time limited)
- [ ] Office door / availability signals in place

### 2.2 Delegation
- [ ] Tasks below your hourly value identified for delegation
- [ ] Delegation plan created (who does what)
- [ ] Systems for delegated tasks documented
- [ ] Follow-up and quality control for delegated work
- [ ] Budget allocated for delegation

### 2.3 Automation
- [ ] Repetitive communications automated (templates, autoresponders)
- [ ] Scheduling automated (online calendar booking)
- [ ] Routine decisions documented for others to make
- [ ] Reporting automated where possible
- [ ] Follow-up sequences automated

---

## STEP 3: Productive Time Architecture

### 3.1 Peak Performance Time
- [ ] Most productive hours identified (when you do your best work)
- [ ] Peak hours reserved for highest-value activities
- [ ] Low-energy times used for administrative tasks
- [ ] No meetings during peak productive hours
- [ ] Environment optimized for focus during peak time

### 3.2 Time Blocking
- [ ] Daily schedule time-blocked (not just task-listed)
- [ ] Revenue-generating activities have dedicated blocks
- [ ] Marketing and sales have weekly dedicated time
- [ ] Administrative tasks batched together
- [ ] Buffer time included (things take longer than planned)

---

## Validation
- [ ] Time vampires identified across all 4 categories
- [ ] Specific elimination or reduction plan for each
- [ ] Boundaries established and communicated
- [ ] Delegation and automation plans in place
- [ ] Weekly time audit scheduled to maintain discipline

---

## Scoring
- 30-34/34 = Time is protected and productive — maintain
- 24-29/34 = Good time management — plug remaining leaks
- 16-23/34 = Significant time being wasted — implement boundaries
- Below 16 = Time is out of control — emergency restructure needed
