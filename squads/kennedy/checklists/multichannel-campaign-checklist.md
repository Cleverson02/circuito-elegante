# Multichannel Campaign Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-media
> Source: No B.S. Direct Marketing + Ultimate Marketing Plan — Multichannel Imperative
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When designing multi-channel direct response campaigns

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Campaign objective and offer defined
- [ ] Target audience channels mapped
- [ ] Budget allocated across channels
- [ ] Tracking systems ready for each channel

---

## STEP 1: Channel Selection
- [ ] Direct mail included (Kennedy's primary channel)
- [ ] Email marketing integrated
- [ ] Phone/call follow-up planned
- [ ] Online (landing pages, social) integrated
- [ ] In-person or event component (if applicable)
- [ ] At least 3 channels active for the campaign

## STEP 2: Channel Coordination
- [ ] Each channel delivers the same core message (consistency)
- [ ] Each channel has unique creative (not identical copy everywhere)
- [ ] Channels are sequenced strategically (mail first, email follow-up)
- [ ] Timing coordinated (all channels hit within the campaign window)
- [ ] No channel contradicts another's message

## STEP 3: DR Compliance Per Channel
- [ ] Every channel has a specific offer
- [ ] Every channel has a deadline/urgency
- [ ] Every channel has a clear CTA
- [ ] Every channel has unique tracking
- [ ] Every channel drives to response (not just awareness)

## STEP 4: Measurement
- [ ] Each channel tracked independently
- [ ] Cross-channel attribution considered
- [ ] ROI calculated per channel
- [ ] Overall campaign ROI calculated
- [ ] Winners and losers identified for next campaign

---

## Scoring
- 20-22/22 = Integrated multichannel DR campaign — deploy
- 16-19/22 = Strong campaign — close integration gaps
- 10-15/22 = Partial multichannel — missing channels or coordination
- Below 10 = Not yet multichannel — expand beyond single channel
