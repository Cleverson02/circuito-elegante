# Preponderance of Proof Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-offers
> Source: The Ultimate Marketing Plan — Proof Architecture
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When assembling proof elements that make claims undeniable

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Claims and promises identified for the offer
- [ ] Access to customer data and results
- [ ] Permission to use testimonials and case studies

---

## PROOF TYPE 1: Testimonials
- [ ] Minimum 5 testimonials with full names and locations
- [ ] Photos included where possible
- [ ] Video testimonials captured for key clients
- [ ] Testimonials address specific results (not generic "great service")
- [ ] Testimonials from relatable people in the target market

## PROOF TYPE 2: Case Studies
- [ ] At least 2 detailed case studies with before/after
- [ ] Specific numbers and metrics cited
- [ ] Timeline documented (how long to achieve results)
- [ ] Client's situation relatable to prospects
- [ ] Process described (not just the outcome)

## PROOF TYPE 3: Demonstrations
- [ ] Product demonstration available (video, sample, trial)
- [ ] Before/after photos or comparisons
- [ ] Live demonstrations at events or in presentations
- [ ] Side-by-side competitor comparison

## PROOF TYPE 4: Credentials
- [ ] Professional certifications or qualifications stated
- [ ] Years of experience quantified
- [ ] Number of clients served or projects completed
- [ ] Education or training relevant to the claim
- [ ] Awards or industry recognition

## PROOF TYPE 5: Third-Party Validation
- [ ] Media mentions or press coverage cited
- [ ] Expert endorsements included
- [ ] Industry association memberships
- [ ] Published research or data referenced
- [ ] "As seen in" logos displayed

## PROOF TYPE 6: Statistics and Data
- [ ] Specific data points supporting claims
- [ ] Industry statistics that validate the problem
- [ ] Your own performance data shared
- [ ] Comparative data (your results vs. industry average)
- [ ] Sources cited for all statistics

## PROOF TYPE 7: Social Proof
- [ ] Number of customers or users stated
- [ ] Client logos displayed (with permission)
- [ ] Community or membership size mentioned
- [ ] Social media engagement as evidence of trust
- [ ] Waiting list or sold-out history referenced

---

## PREPONDERANCE THRESHOLD
Kennedy's standard: Use so much proof that any reasonable person would have to agree.

- [ ] At least 4 of 7 proof types represented
- [ ] Proof elements distributed throughout the marketing piece (not just one section)
- [ ] Each major claim has its own proof support
- [ ] Total proof creates an overwhelming weight of evidence
- [ ] A skeptic would be unable to dismiss ALL proof types

---

## Scoring
- 30-34/34 = Overwhelming proof — undeniable claims
- 24-29/34 = Strong proof — add more variety
- 18-23/34 = Moderate proof — several types missing
- Below 18 = Insufficient proof — claims are unsupported
