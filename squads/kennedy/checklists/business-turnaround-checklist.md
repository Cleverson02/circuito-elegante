# Business Turnaround Checklist — 8-Step System

> Agent: kennedy-business
> Source: The Ultimate No B.S. Business Success Book — Turnaround Methodology
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When a business is in decline, stagnation, or crisis

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Financial statements for last 12 months available
- [ ] Customer data and marketing metrics gathered
- [ ] Team/staffing information documented
- [ ] Willingness to make difficult decisions confirmed

---

## STEP 1: Stop the Bleeding
- [ ] Identify the 3 biggest cash drains
- [ ] Cut all non-essential expenses immediately
- [ ] Eliminate marketing that is not producing measurable results
- [ ] Renegotiate vendor contracts where possible
- [ ] Freeze all hiring (evaluate existing team first)

## STEP 2: Cash Flow Emergency Protocol
- [ ] Activate immediate revenue generators (see Five Keys to Cash Flow)
- [ ] Contact existing customers with compelling new offer
- [ ] Reactivate lost customers with special comeback deal
- [ ] Collect all outstanding receivables aggressively
- [ ] Liquidate obsolete inventory or unused assets

## STEP 3: Marketing Audit
- [ ] Classify all marketing as DR or Institutional
- [ ] Kill all institutional spending immediately
- [ ] Redirect budget to highest-performing DR channels
- [ ] Implement tracking for every remaining marketing dollar
- [ ] Results Triangle re-evaluated (Message-Market-Media)

## STEP 4: Customer Base Analysis
- [ ] Identify top 20% of customers by revenue/profit
- [ ] Identify bottom 20% of customers by hassle/cost
- [ ] Create special retention program for top customers
- [ ] Graduate or fire bottom customers
- [ ] Calculate true customer lifetime value

## STEP 5: Offer Reconstruction
- [ ] Current offers evaluated for irresistibility
- [ ] USP reassessed (is it still unique and compelling?)
- [ ] Pricing reviewed (underpriced? overpriced? no premium tier?)
- [ ] New "turnaround offer" created (fresh, compelling, urgent)
- [ ] Guarantee redesigned to be boldest in industry

## STEP 6: Team Assessment
- [ ] Each team member evaluated for contribution vs. cost
- [ ] "A" players identified and protected
- [ ] "C" players given 30-day improvement deadline
- [ ] "D" players terminated immediately
- [ ] Key roles that need filling identified

## STEP 7: System Repair
- [ ] Follow-up system rebuilt (leads falling through cracks?)
- [ ] Customer communication reinstated (newsletter, regular contact)
- [ ] Sales process documented and optimized
- [ ] Referral system reactivated or built
- [ ] Operations streamlined (what can be automated or eliminated?)

## STEP 8: Accelerate
- [ ] 90-day turnaround plan documented with milestones
- [ ] Weekly review cadence established
- [ ] Quick-win campaigns launched (revenue within 30 days)
- [ ] Long-term DR marketing plan in development
- [ ] Accountability partner or advisor engaged

---

## EMERGENCY REVENUE TACTICS (Deploy Immediately)

### Week 1 Actions
- [ ] Lost customer reactivation letter sent
- [ ] Existing customer appreciation + upsell offer sent
- [ ] Referral request campaign launched
- [ ] One new lead generation campaign activated
- [ ] Outstanding invoices collected

### Week 2-4 Actions
- [ ] Results from Week 1 measured
- [ ] Second round of customer reactivation
- [ ] New offer tested
- [ ] Marketing systems rebuilt or repaired
- [ ] Team changes implemented

---

## Validation
- [ ] All 8 steps addressed with specific actions
- [ ] Cash flow emergency stabilized
- [ ] 90-day plan documented
- [ ] Weekly review scheduled
- [ ] Revenue trend reversed or stabilizing

---

## Scoring
- 38-42/42 = Comprehensive turnaround plan — execute with discipline
- 30-37/42 = Strong plan — fill remaining gaps
- 22-29/42 = Partial plan — critical steps missing
- Below 22 = Still in crisis mode — address fundamentals first
