# Emotional Copy Architecture Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-copy
> Source: Copywriting Academy + Mind Hijacking Course — Emotional Trigger System
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When writing or auditing copy for emotional effectiveness

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Target audience emotional landscape mapped
- [ ] Key desires, fears, and frustrations identified
- [ ] Product/service transformation clearly defined
- [ ] Copy draft available for review

---

## STEP 1: Fear-Based Emotions

### 1.1 Fear of Loss
- [ ] What the prospect will LOSE by not acting is clearly stated
- [ ] Loss is specific and personal (not abstract)
- [ ] Loss is painted vividly (reader can feel it)
- [ ] Fear of loss is stronger than promise of gain in key sections

### 1.2 Fear of Missing Out
- [ ] Others are already benefiting (social proof creates FOMO)
- [ ] Limited availability or deadline creates scarcity
- [ ] Specific consequences of delay described
- [ ] Reader feels urgency without feeling manipulated

### 1.3 Fear of Embarrassment
- [ ] Social consequences of inaction highlighted
- [ ] "What will others think?" gently activated
- [ ] Status preservation motivation triggered
- [ ] Fear balanced with empowerment (not just shame)

---

## STEP 2: Desire-Based Emotions

### 2.1 Hope and Aspiration
- [ ] Future-paced result painted in vivid detail
- [ ] Reader can SEE themselves in the transformed state
- [ ] Hope is grounded in proof (not just promises)
- [ ] Aspiration feels achievable (not fantasy)

### 2.2 Greed / Self-Interest
- [ ] Financial or personal gains clearly quantified
- [ ] "What's in it for me?" answered thoroughly
- [ ] Value proposition makes reader feel they are getting a deal
- [ ] Investment vs. cost framing used

### 2.3 Pride and Status
- [ ] Ownership of the product/service elevates identity
- [ ] Association with success or elite group implied
- [ ] Reader feels smarter or more sophisticated for considering
- [ ] Social currency of the purchase highlighted

---

## STEP 3: Trust-Building Emotions

### 3.1 Authority
- [ ] Credentials and expertise established early
- [ ] Expert endorsements or institutional credibility
- [ ] Track record of results demonstrated
- [ ] Reader feels they are in safe, competent hands

### 3.2 Empathy
- [ ] Writer demonstrates understanding of reader's situation
- [ ] "I've been there" or "I understand" moments
- [ ] Reader's frustrations validated (not dismissed)
- [ ] Connection before persuasion

### 3.3 Belonging
- [ ] Community or tribe membership implied
- [ ] "People like you" language used
- [ ] Testimonials from relatable people
- [ ] Reader feels they are joining something, not just buying something

---

## STEP 4: Emotional Architecture

### 4.1 Emotional Flow
- [ ] Copy starts with the reader's current emotional state
- [ ] Emotions escalate through the copy (not flat)
- [ ] Positive and negative emotions alternate (not all doom or all hype)
- [ ] Emotional peak occurs right before the call to action

### 4.2 Story-Emotion Integration
- [ ] Stories carry the emotional weight (not just claims)
- [ ] Each story evokes a specific planned emotion
- [ ] Characters in stories are relatable to the reader
- [ ] Emotional resolution of stories points to the product

### 4.3 Balance
- [ ] Emotion supported by logic (emotional decision, logical justification)
- [ ] Not manipulative (genuine, honest emotional appeal)
- [ ] Emotions serve the reader's best interest
- [ ] Emotional copy leads to genuine value delivery

---

## Validation
- [ ] At least 3 fear-based triggers activated
- [ ] At least 3 desire-based triggers activated
- [ ] Trust emotions established before asking for action
- [ ] Emotional arc builds to a peak before CTA
- [ ] Copy tested on 3 people: did they FEEL something?

---

## Scoring
- 30-34/34 = Emotionally powerful copy — deploy
- 24-29/34 = Strong emotional foundation — intensify weak areas
- 18-23/34 = Some emotion but flat — needs more trigger depth
- Below 18 = Copy is logical but not emotional — rewrite with triggers
