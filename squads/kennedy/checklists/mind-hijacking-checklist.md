# Mind Hijacking Campaign Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-persuasion
> Source: Mind Hijacking Course — Psychological Trigger Architecture
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When designing persuasion campaigns using Kennedy's mind hijacking triggers

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Target audience psychological profile completed
- [ ] Product/service clearly defined
- [ ] Ethical boundaries established (persuasion, not manipulation)
- [ ] Delivery channel selected

---

## STEP 1: Pattern Interrupt

### 1.1 Attention Capture
- [ ] Pattern interrupt designed (breaks the autopilot state)
- [ ] First impression is unexpected or startling
- [ ] Interrupt is relevant to the message (not random shock)
- [ ] Interrupt transitions naturally into the message

### 1.2 Pattern Interrupt Types Used
- [ ] Visual interrupt (unexpected imagery or format)
- [ ] Verbal interrupt (shocking or contradictory opening statement)
- [ ] Logical interrupt (contradicts expected information)
- [ ] Physical interrupt (lumpy mail, dimensional package)
- [ ] Social interrupt (startling social proof statistic)
- [ ] At least 2 interrupt types deployed

---

## STEP 2: Fascination Triggers

### 2.1 Curiosity Loop
- [ ] Open loop created that MUST be closed by reading/watching more
- [ ] Information gap is specific (not generic curiosity)
- [ ] Loop cannot be resolved without engaging with the content
- [ ] Multiple loops layered throughout the piece

### 2.2 Fascination Elements
- [ ] Secrets revealed (insider knowledge promised)
- [ ] Contradictions exposed (challenges what they believe)
- [ ] Specificity used (exact numbers, names, details)
- [ ] Story hooks deployed (narrative that must be completed)
- [ ] Minimum 5 fascination triggers per piece

---

## STEP 3: Emotional Trigger Architecture

### 3.1 Fear-Based Triggers
- [ ] Loss aversion activated (what they will lose by NOT acting)
- [ ] Fear of missing out (FOMO) created with real scarcity
- [ ] Future pain painted vividly (what happens if nothing changes)
- [ ] Fear is specific and personal (not abstract)

### 3.2 Desire-Based Triggers
- [ ] Future self visualization (life AFTER the purchase)
- [ ] Status and identity appeal (who they become)
- [ ] Freedom and autonomy promised
- [ ] Specific pleasure described in sensory terms

### 3.3 Trust Triggers
- [ ] Authority established (credentials, achievements, media)
- [ ] Social proof deployed (testimonials, numbers, case studies)
- [ ] Consistency/commitment leveraged (small yeses lead to big yes)
- [ ] Reciprocity activated (give value before asking)
- [ ] Liking factor created (personal connection, shared values)

---

## STEP 4: Objection Pre-emption

### 4.1 Mind Reading
- [ ] Top 5 objections identified before they arise
- [ ] Each objection addressed within the copy (not after)
- [ ] "I know what you're thinking..." technique used
- [ ] Objections reframed as reasons TO buy

### 4.2 Damaging Admission
- [ ] Honest limitation acknowledged (builds credibility)
- [ ] Admission is followed by a stronger positive
- [ ] Admission differentiates from competitors who claim perfection
- [ ] Reader thinks "at least they're honest"

---

## STEP 5: Action Architecture

### 5.1 Decision Simplification
- [ ] Choice architecture designed (make buying the easy path)
- [ ] Decision paralysis prevented (clear recommendation)
- [ ] Default option is the purchase (inertia works in your favor)
- [ ] Buying path has fewer steps than NOT buying

### 5.2 Commitment Escalation
- [ ] Small commitments lead to larger ones (foot in the door)
- [ ] Each micro-commitment deepens engagement
- [ ] Final purchase feels like natural next step
- [ ] Post-purchase reinforcement planned (reduce buyer's remorse)

---

## ETHICAL BOUNDARIES

### Non-Negotiable Standards
- [ ] All claims are truthful and verifiable
- [ ] Scarcity and urgency are genuine (not manufactured)
- [ ] Product/service delivers on its promises
- [ ] Target audience benefits from the purchase
- [ ] Tactics serve the customer, not just the seller

---

## Validation
- [ ] All 5 steps have active components
- [ ] Ethical boundaries maintained throughout
- [ ] Campaign tested on 3-5 target audience members
- [ ] Emotional and logical triggers balanced
- [ ] Call to action is clear and compelling

---

## Scoring
- 38-42/42 = Powerful persuasion campaign — deploy
- 30-37/42 = Strong campaign — strengthen weak triggers
- 22-29/42 = Needs more trigger layers
- Below 22 = Campaign is not yet persuasive — rebuild
