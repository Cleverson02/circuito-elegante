# Affluent Client Positioning Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-affluent
> Source: Affluent Client Attraction Playbook — Premium Positioning System
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When positioning a business or offer to attract affluent clients

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Current client base analyzed (income/net worth distribution)
- [ ] Affluent target market defined
- [ ] Competitor positioning researched
- [ ] Willingness to eliminate non-affluent marketing

---

## STEP 1: Affluent Psychology Understanding

### 1.1 Mindset Mapping
- [ ] Affluent time-value equation understood (they pay money to save time)
- [ ] Status and identity drivers identified for your niche
- [ ] Decision-making process mapped (fast, delegate, trust-based)
- [ ] Risk perception understood (fear of wasting time > fear of losing money)

### 1.2 Value Perception
- [ ] Higher price = higher quality perception leveraged
- [ ] Exclusivity and scarcity built into the positioning
- [ ] White-glove service expectation addressed
- [ ] Access and privacy expectations considered

---

## STEP 2: Premium Positioning Elements

### 2.1 Price Positioning
- [ ] Prices significantly above market average (no apology)
- [ ] Price anchored against even higher alternatives
- [ ] No discounting or negotiating (price integrity maintained)
- [ ] Payment terms that match affluent preferences (not just lowest price)

### 2.2 Experience Design
- [ ] Every touchpoint signals premium quality
- [ ] Speed and convenience prioritized over cost savings
- [ ] Personal attention and customization offered
- [ ] Physical environment (if applicable) reflects affluent taste

### 2.3 Access and Exclusivity
- [ ] Application or qualification process for clients
- [ ] Limited availability communicated (not for everyone)
- [ ] VIP or inner circle tier available
- [ ] Waiting list creates desire (not frustration)

---

## STEP 3: Marketing to the Affluent

### 3.1 Language and Tone
- [ ] No discount language ("save," "cheap," "affordable" eliminated)
- [ ] Investment language used ("investment," "value," "exclusive")
- [ ] Sophistication without pretension
- [ ] Speaks to identity and lifestyle, not just features

### 3.2 Proof Elements
- [ ] Testimonials from recognizable or affluent clients
- [ ] Case studies featuring high-net-worth outcomes
- [ ] Media coverage in publications affluent people read
- [ ] Association with other premium brands or institutions

### 3.3 Channel Selection
- [ ] Direct mail to qualified lists (not mass marketing)
- [ ] Private events and exclusive gatherings
- [ ] Referrals from trusted advisors (attorneys, CPAs, financial planners)
- [ ] Content in publications the affluent consume

---

## STEP 4: Operational Alignment

### 4.1 Delivery Matches Promise
- [ ] Service quality actually matches premium positioning
- [ ] Staff trained to serve affluent clients appropriately
- [ ] Response times are faster than industry standard
- [ ] Problems are resolved immediately with over-compensation

### 4.2 Relationship Management
- [ ] Personal relationship maintained (not just transactional)
- [ ] Regular communication adds value (not just sells)
- [ ] Special occasions recognized (birthdays, anniversaries)
- [ ] Client feels known and valued as an individual

---

## Validation
- [ ] All marketing materials pass the "would an affluent person engage?" test
- [ ] Pricing reflects premium positioning (not middle-market)
- [ ] At least 3 exclusivity signals present in marketing
- [ ] Operations can deliver on the premium promise
- [ ] Existing affluent clients would recommend you to peers

---

## Scoring
- 30-34/34 = True affluent positioning — attract with confidence
- 24-29/34 = Strong positioning — fill the gaps
- 18-23/34 = Mixed signals — some elements premium, some mass-market
- Below 18 = Not yet positioned for affluent — significant work needed
