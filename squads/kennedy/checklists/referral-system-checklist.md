# 11-Point Referral System Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-referrals
> Source: The Ultimate No BS Referral Machine — 11-Point Referral System
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When building or auditing a systematic referral generation machine

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Existing customer base of at least 25 active customers
- [ ] Customer satisfaction confirmed (NPS > 7 or equivalent)
- [ ] Referral incentive budget allocated
- [ ] CRM or tracking system available

---

## POINT 1: Set Expectations from Day One
- [ ] New customers are told referrals are expected (part of the relationship)
- [ ] Referral expectation included in onboarding materials
- [ ] Framed as a commitment, not just a nice-to-have
- [ ] Language: "We grow through referrals from happy clients like you"

## POINT 2: Create a WOW Experience
- [ ] Customer experience exceeds expectations at every touchpoint
- [ ] At least one "surprise and delight" moment in the journey
- [ ] Service recovery system handles problems faster than expected
- [ ] Customers actively comment on how different the experience is

## POINT 3: Ask at the Right Moment
- [ ] Referral request timed after peak satisfaction moment
- [ ] NOT asked at the point of sale (too early)
- [ ] Asked after successful delivery, result, or milestone
- [ ] System identifies optimal asking moments automatically

## POINT 4: Make It Easy to Refer
- [ ] Pre-written referral scripts or templates provided
- [ ] Physical referral cards or digital sharing tools available
- [ ] Customer does not have to "sell" you — materials do the work
- [ ] One-click or one-step referral process (zero friction)

## POINT 5: Provide Incentives (Both Sides)
- [ ] Referrer receives a meaningful reward (not just "thank you")
- [ ] Referred person receives a special welcome offer
- [ ] Incentives are tangible and immediate (not points or future credits)
- [ ] Incentive structure is communicated clearly

## POINT 6: Endorsed Mailing System
- [ ] Current customers send pre-written letters to their contacts
- [ ] Letters include personal endorsement from the customer
- [ ] Materials provided (letters, envelopes, stamps if needed)
- [ ] Timing coordinated with customer's highest satisfaction moment

## POINT 7: Champion Circle of Influence
- [ ] Top 10% of referrers identified as "Champions"
- [ ] Champions receive special recognition and elevated incentives
- [ ] Regular communication maintains Champion engagement
- [ ] Champions are treated as unofficial ambassadors

## POINT 8: Strategic Alliances
- [ ] Non-competing businesses with same target market identified
- [ ] Cross-referral agreements established
- [ ] Joint ventures or co-marketing planned
- [ ] Alliance partners receive referral incentives too

## POINT 9: Testimonial Machine
- [ ] Systematic testimonial collection process in place
- [ ] Video testimonials captured when possible
- [ ] Testimonials used across all marketing (with permission)
- [ ] Testimonials reference specific results and experiences

## POINT 10: Lost Customer Reactivation
- [ ] Lost or lapsed customers identified (inactive 90+ days)
- [ ] "We miss you" campaign designed and scheduled
- [ ] Special comeback offer created for lapsed customers
- [ ] Win-back campaign runs quarterly at minimum

## POINT 11: Measure and Optimize
- [ ] Referral rate tracked monthly (referrals per customer)
- [ ] Cost per referred customer calculated
- [ ] Lifetime value of referred vs. non-referred customers compared
- [ ] Referral sources identified (which customers refer most)
- [ ] System is reviewed and optimized quarterly

---

## Validation
- [ ] All 11 points addressed (even if some are in development)
- [ ] At least 7 of 11 points actively operational
- [ ] Referral rate is tracked and trending upward
- [ ] System runs without owner's daily involvement
- [ ] Referrals account for measurable percentage of new business

---

## Scoring
- 40-44/44 = Complete referral machine — optimize and scale
- 32-39/44 = Strong system — activate remaining points
- 22-31/44 = Partial system — major points missing
- Below 22 = Referral generation is ad hoc, not systematic
