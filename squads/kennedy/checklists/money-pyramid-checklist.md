# Money Pyramid Analysis Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-audit
> Source: No B.S. Direct Marketing — Money Pyramid Market Segmentation
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When analyzing market positioning and pricing strategy

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Current customer base data available (revenue per customer)
- [ ] Industry pricing benchmarks researched
- [ ] Competitor positioning mapped

---

## The Money Pyramid

```
        /\
       /  \    1% Ultra-Rich ($10M+ net worth)
      /____\
     /      \   4% Rich ($1M-$10M net worth)
    /________\
   /          \  15% Upper-Middle ($200K-$1M income)
  /____________\
 /              \ 60% Middle/Working (paycheck to paycheck)
/________________\
       20% Broke (below poverty/debt)
```

---

## STEP 1: Current Position Assessment

### 1.1 Where Are You Now?
- [ ] Identified which pyramid segment your current customers occupy
- [ ] Calculated average revenue per customer
- [ ] Determined what percentage of customers are in each segment
- [ ] Identified your most profitable segment

### 1.2 Revenue Concentration
- [ ] Know what percentage of revenue comes from top 20% of customers
- [ ] Identified if you have any ultra-rich or rich clients
- [ ] Mapped the relationship between segment and profitability
- [ ] Determined if you are "selling down" instead of "selling up"

---

## STEP 2: Pyramid Movement Strategy

### 2.1 Moving Up the Pyramid
- [ ] Premium tier or VIP offering created
- [ ] Marketing language adjusted for higher segments
- [ ] Service delivery upgraded for premium clients
- [ ] Pricing reflects higher pyramid positioning

### 2.2 Segment-Specific Marketing
- [ ] Each targeted segment has specific marketing approach
- [ ] Messaging adjusted for segment psychology
- [ ] Media selection matches segment consumption habits
- [ ] Offers designed for segment-specific desires

### 2.3 Access Strategy
- [ ] Know how to reach each pyramid segment
- [ ] Lists and databases identified for target segments
- [ ] Referral networks into higher segments established
- [ ] Events and environments where target segment gathers identified

---

## STEP 3: Pricing Alignment

### 3.1 Price-Pyramid Match
- [ ] Prices appropriate for targeted pyramid segment
- [ ] Not underpriced for the segment (signals low quality to higher segments)
- [ ] Not overpriced for the segment (excludes without reason)
- [ ] Price positioning intentionally chosen (not default)

### 3.2 Value Ladder
- [ ] Entry-level offer for segment below target (feeder)
- [ ] Core offer for target segment
- [ ] Premium offer for segment above target (aspirational)
- [ ] Each tier has clear value differentiation

---

## STEP 4: Implementation

### 4.1 Quick Wins
- [ ] Identified 1-3 immediate actions to serve current best segment better
- [ ] Created or upgraded premium offering
- [ ] Adjusted marketing message for higher segment
- [ ] Implemented at least one "selling up" strategy

### 4.2 Long-Term Strategy
- [ ] 12-month plan for pyramid movement documented
- [ ] Marketing budget reallocated toward target segment
- [ ] Team trained on serving higher pyramid segments
- [ ] Measurement system tracks segment migration

---

## Validation
- [ ] Current pyramid position clearly identified
- [ ] Target pyramid position defined
- [ ] Strategy for upward movement documented
- [ ] Pricing aligned with target segment
- [ ] At least one action taken toward pyramid movement

---

## Scoring
- 22-26/26 = Clear pyramid strategy — execute with discipline
- 18-21/26 = Good awareness — formalize the strategy
- 12-17/26 = Partial understanding — significant strategy gaps
- Below 12 = No pyramid awareness — complete analysis needed
