# DR Social Media Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-media
> Source: No B.S. Direct Response Social Media Marketing (2015)
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When auditing or designing social media for direct response (not vanity)

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Social media accounts active on relevant platforms
- [ ] Target market's social media habits identified
- [ ] Content creation capacity assessed
- [ ] Tracking and analytics tools in place

---

## KENNEDY'S CORE PRINCIPLE

Social media is a TOOL for DR, not a replacement for it. If it cannot be measured and attributed to revenue, it is a hobby. Social media without direct response principles is the digital equivalent of institutional advertising.

---

## STEP 1: DR Compliance on Social Media

### 1.1 Every Post Has Purpose
- [ ] Posts drive to a specific action (click, opt-in, call)
- [ ] Content links to DR landing pages (not just homepage)
- [ ] Call to action included in posts (not just "engagement bait")
- [ ] Tracking links used (UTM parameters or unique URLs)

### 1.2 Offer-Driven Content
- [ ] Regular offers presented through social channels
- [ ] Lead magnets promoted consistently
- [ ] Social-exclusive offers created (trackable to social)
- [ ] Offers have deadlines (not perpetually available)

### 1.3 Measurement
- [ ] Revenue attributed to social media efforts
- [ ] Cost per lead from social calculated
- [ ] Cost per acquisition from social calculated
- [ ] Social ROI compared to other DR channels
- [ ] Vanity metrics (likes, follows) NOT the primary KPI

---

## STEP 2: Content Strategy

### 2.1 Value-First Content
- [ ] 80% of content provides genuine value
- [ ] Content demonstrates expertise and authority
- [ ] Stories and case studies shared regularly
- [ ] Content educates while selling (not just selling)

### 2.2 DR-Enhanced Content
- [ ] Value content includes soft CTAs (learn more, get the guide)
- [ ] Content creates curiosity about paid offerings
- [ ] Content showcases results and transformations
- [ ] Content builds trust that leads to conversion

### 2.3 Social Proof
- [ ] Customer testimonials shared regularly
- [ ] Before/after results showcased
- [ ] Client success stories highlighted
- [ ] User-generated content encouraged and shared

---

## STEP 3: Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### 3.1 Social Media Sins (Kennedy)
- [ ] NOT posting for the sake of posting (no content without purpose)
- [ ] NOT chasing followers over customers
- [ ] NOT spending hours on engagement without conversion
- [ ] NOT treating social media as free advertising (time has cost)
- [ ] NOT outsourcing to social media "experts" who measure likes

### 3.2 Platform Traps
- [ ] NOT building 100% on rented land (always drive to owned lists)
- [ ] NOT dependent on any single platform's algorithm
- [ ] NOT creating content only for the platform (create for your audience)
- [ ] NOT confusing platform engagement with business results

---

## STEP 4: List Building via Social

### 4.1 Social to Email/Mail
- [ ] Every social profile links to lead magnet or opt-in
- [ ] Regular content drives to email list building
- [ ] Social audience being converted to owned audience
- [ ] Goal: social followers become email/mail subscribers

### 4.2 Retargeting
- [ ] Website visitors from social are retargeted
- [ ] Custom audiences built from email lists
- [ ] Lookalike audiences created from best customers
- [ ] Retargeting serves DR offers (not just "awareness")

---

## Validation
- [ ] Social media ROI calculated (revenue, not just engagement)
- [ ] List-building from social is active and growing
- [ ] No vanity-metric-only campaigns running
- [ ] Social strategy integrates with overall DR marketing plan
- [ ] Time investment in social justified by measurable returns

---

## Scoring
- 28-32/32 = DR-driven social media — continue and scale
- 22-27/32 = Mostly DR — eliminate vanity elements
- 16-21/32 = Mixed — significant institutional social present
- Below 16 = Social media is a hobby, not marketing — overhaul needed
