# Follow-Up System Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-magnetic
> Source: Magnetic Marketing + No B.S. Direct Marketing — Follow-Up Imperative
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When building or auditing a multi-step follow-up system

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Lead capture system in place
- [ ] CRM or contact management tool available
- [ ] Email service provider configured
- [ ] Follow-up content planned or available

---

## STEP 1: Immediate Response (0-24 Hours)

### 1.1 Speed of Response
- [ ] Lead receives acknowledgment within 5 minutes (automated)
- [ ] Personal outreach (call/email) within 4 hours during business hours
- [ ] Shock and Awe package ships within 24 hours
- [ ] Pre-arrival notification sent (email/text)

### 1.2 First Touch Content
- [ ] Thank-you message sent immediately
- [ ] Lead magnet or promised content delivered
- [ ] Expectation set for next contact (what happens next)
- [ ] Emergency contact info provided (show you are available)

---

## STEP 2: Short-Term Follow-Up (Days 1-14)

### 2.1 Multi-Touch Sequence
- [ ] Day 1: Initial contact + lead magnet delivery
- [ ] Day 2: "Did you receive?" check-in
- [ ] Day 3-4: Value-add content (article, video, resource)
- [ ] Day 5-7: Case study or testimonial showcase
- [ ] Day 8-10: Urgency or limited-time offer
- [ ] Day 12-14: Final "the door is closing" message

### 2.2 Multi-Channel Delivery
- [ ] At least 3 different channels used (email, mail, phone, text, social)
- [ ] Each channel delivers unique content (not same message everywhere)
- [ ] Channel sequence designed strategically (not random)
- [ ] Physical mail included (not just digital)

---

## STEP 3: Medium-Term Follow-Up (Days 15-90)

### 3.1 Nurture Sequence
- [ ] Weekly value-add content continues
- [ ] Different angles and offers presented
- [ ] Stories and case studies rotated
- [ ] Objections addressed one at a time
- [ ] Re-engagement offers for non-responders

### 3.2 Segmentation
- [ ] Engaged leads receive accelerated sequence
- [ ] Partially engaged receive different track
- [ ] Cold leads receive periodic reactivation attempts
- [ ] Buyers immediately moved to customer sequence

---

## STEP 4: Long-Term Follow-Up (90+ Days)

### 4.1 Perpetual Contact
- [ ] Monthly newsletter in place (print preferred)
- [ ] Quarterly special offers or events
- [ ] Annual "state of the union" communication
- [ ] Lead NEVER drops off unless they explicitly unsubscribe/ask to stop

### 4.2 Reactivation Campaigns
- [ ] "We noticed you haven't..." emails for dormant leads
- [ ] Special comeback offer for long-dormant leads
- [ ] "Last chance" or "closing your file" messages
- [ ] Birthday or anniversary-triggered outreach

---

## STEP 5: System Architecture

### 5.1 Automation
- [ ] Sequences are automated (not manual/memory-dependent)
- [ ] Triggers are set for key actions (opt-in, click, purchase)
- [ ] Segmentation happens automatically based on behavior
- [ ] System runs 24/7 without human intervention for routine steps

### 5.2 Measurement
- [ ] Open rates tracked per step
- [ ] Click rates tracked per step
- [ ] Conversion tracked per step (which step produces buyers?)
- [ ] Drop-off points identified and addressed
- [ ] Overall sequence conversion rate known

---

## Validation
- [ ] No lead falls through the cracks (system catches everyone)
- [ ] Follow-up continues until: buy, die, or tell you to stop
- [ ] Multiple channels used (not email-only)
- [ ] System runs without daily owner involvement
- [ ] Follow-up adds value at every touchpoint (not just "checking in")

---

## Scoring
- 34-38/38 = Complete follow-up machine — optimize and scale
- 28-33/38 = Strong system — add missing elements
- 20-27/38 = Partial system — major gaps in follow-up
- Below 20 = No real system — leads are being lost
