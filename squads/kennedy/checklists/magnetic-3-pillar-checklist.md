# Magnetic Marketing 3-Pillar System Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-magnetic
> Source: Magnetic Marketing (2018) — 3-Pillar System (Attract-Convert-Retain)
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When building or auditing a complete magnetic marketing system

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Business model and revenue targets defined
- [ ] Target market identified
- [ ] Current marketing spend documented
- [ ] Results Triangle completed (Message-Market-Media)

---

## PILLAR 1: ATTRACTION — Getting Them to Raise Their Hand

### 1.1 Lead Magnet
- [ ] Irresistible lead magnet created (report, guide, toolkit, video)
- [ ] Lead magnet solves a specific, urgent problem
- [ ] Lead magnet has a compelling title (not generic)
- [ ] Lead magnet demonstrates expertise without giving away everything
- [ ] Lead magnet can be consumed in 15-30 minutes

### 1.2 Lead Generation Campaign
- [ ] Multiple lead generation channels active
- [ ] Advertising uses DR principles (offer, deadline, CTA)
- [ ] Landing pages or response mechanisms in place
- [ ] Tracking in place for every lead source
- [ ] Cost per lead known for each channel

### 1.3 Pattern Interrupt
- [ ] Marketing stands out from competitor noise
- [ ] Headlines are provocative and attention-grabbing
- [ ] Something about the approach is unexpected
- [ ] First impression is memorable and different

---

## PILLAR 2: CONVERSION — Turning Prospects into Customers

### 2.1 Shock and Awe Package
- [ ] Comprehensive information package designed
- [ ] Package overwhelms with proof and value
- [ ] Sent immediately upon lead capture (speed matters)
- [ ] Includes testimonials, case studies, credentials
- [ ] Includes a clear next-step offer

### 2.2 Follow-Up System
- [ ] Automated follow-up sequence in place (minimum 7 touches)
- [ ] Follow-up uses multiple media (mail, email, phone, text)
- [ ] Each follow-up adds new value or angle (not just "checking in")
- [ ] Follow-up continues until prospect buys, dies, or tells you to stop
- [ ] No lead falls through the cracks

### 2.3 Sales Process
- [ ] Clear sales presentation or page designed
- [ ] Objection handling prepared for top 5 objections
- [ ] Risk reversal (guarantee) prominently featured
- [ ] Urgency mechanism built into conversion process
- [ ] Multiple payment options available

---

## PILLAR 3: RETENTION — Keeping Them Buying

### 3.1 Customer Communication
- [ ] Monthly newsletter in place (print preferred)
- [ ] Regular value-add communication (not just promotions)
- [ ] Personal relationship maintained (birthday cards, thank-you notes)
- [ ] Customer feels like part of a community or "inner circle"

### 3.2 Ascension Model
- [ ] Upsell/cross-sell offers designed for existing customers
- [ ] Clear value ladder or ascension path
- [ ] Premium tier or VIP offering available
- [ ] Regular new offers presented to existing customers

### 3.3 Referral System
- [ ] Referral request system in place (ask systematically, not randomly)
- [ ] Referral incentive offered (both referrer and referred)
- [ ] Endorsed mailing program active (or planned)
- [ ] Testimonial collection system active

### 3.4 Reactivation
- [ ] Lost customer reactivation campaign designed
- [ ] "We miss you" sequence for lapsed customers
- [ ] Special "comeback" offer for former customers
- [ ] Regular review of customer base for at-risk accounts

---

## SYSTEM INTEGRATION

### Pillar Connection
- [ ] Attraction feeds directly into Conversion (no gap)
- [ ] Conversion experience sets up Retention
- [ ] Retention creates referrals that feed Attraction
- [ ] All three pillars share consistent messaging
- [ ] System is automated where possible

### Measurement
- [ ] Cost per lead tracked (Pillar 1)
- [ ] Conversion rate tracked (Pillar 2)
- [ ] Lifetime customer value tracked (Pillar 3)
- [ ] Referral rate tracked (Pillar 3 to Pillar 1)
- [ ] Overall system ROI calculated

---

## Validation
- [ ] All 3 pillars have active, working components
- [ ] No "one-legged stool" (one pillar vastly stronger than others)
- [ ] System can run without the owner's daily involvement
- [ ] Monthly metrics are tracked and reviewed

---

## Scoring
- 38-42/42 = Complete magnetic marketing machine
- 30-37/42 = Strong system — fill the gaps
- 22-29/42 = Partial system — significant pillars need building
- Below 22 = System not yet functional — build from Pillar 1
