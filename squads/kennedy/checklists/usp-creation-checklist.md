# USP Creation Checklist

> Agent: kennedy-offers
> Source: The Ultimate Marketing Plan (3rd Ed) — USP Construction (4 Pathways)
> Version: 1.0
> Use: When creating or auditing a Unique Selling Proposition

---

## Pre-Requisites
- [ ] Business/product clearly defined
- [ ] Target market identified with specificity
- [ ] Competitor landscape researched
- [ ] Current positioning (if any) documented

---

## STEP 1: The USP Foundation

### 1.1 The "Why You?" Test
- [ ] Can answer "Why should I choose you over every other option?" in one sentence
- [ ] Answer is specific, not generic ("quality" and "service" are NOT a USP)
- [ ] USP is meaningful to the target customer (not just to you)
- [ ] USP would still work if competitor copied your marketing but not your USP

### 1.2 Kennedy's 4 Pathways to USP
- [ ] Pathway 1: Be UNIQUE in what you offer (nobody else has it)
- [ ] Pathway 2: Be UNIQUE in HOW you deliver it (proprietary process)
- [ ] Pathway 3: Be UNIQUE in WHO you serve (hyper-niche specialization)
- [ ] Pathway 4: Be UNIQUE in your GUARANTEE (boldest in the industry)
- [ ] At least ONE pathway selected and developed

---

## STEP 2: USP Crafting

### 2.1 Specificity
- [ ] USP includes a specific number, timeframe, or measurable claim
- [ ] USP avoids vague language ("best," "premier," "leading")
- [ ] USP can be verified or demonstrated
- [ ] USP is concrete enough to be tested

### 2.2 Memorability
- [ ] USP is expressible in one sentence (under 20 words ideal)
- [ ] USP is easy to repeat and remember
- [ ] USP creates a mental picture
- [ ] USP differentiates immediately upon hearing it

### 2.3 Emotional Impact
- [ ] USP addresses a real pain or desire of the target
- [ ] USP triggers an emotional response (relief, excitement, curiosity)
- [ ] USP feels like a solution, not just a claim
- [ ] USP makes the prospect think "that's exactly what I need"

---

## STEP 3: USP Integration

### 3.1 Consistency
- [ ] USP appears on every marketing piece
- [ ] USP is reflected in actual business operations (not just marketing)
- [ ] All employees can articulate the USP
- [ ] USP informs product development decisions

### 3.2 Proof
- [ ] At least 3 proof elements support the USP
- [ ] Testimonials reference the USP claim
- [ ] Case studies demonstrate the USP in action
- [ ] Guarantee backs up the USP promise

---

## STEP 4: The Acid Test

### 4.1 Competitive Differentiation
- [ ] USP cannot be claimed by any current competitor
- [ ] USP creates a category of one
- [ ] If competitor put their name on your ad, it would NOT make sense
- [ ] USP gives customers a reason to PAY MORE

### 4.2 Customer Validation
- [ ] 5 target customers shown USP and asked for reaction
- [ ] At least 3 of 5 found it compelling and different
- [ ] Nobody confused by what it means
- [ ] USP answered their "why should I care?" question

---

## Validation
- [ ] USP passes the "cocktail party test" (easy to explain and interesting)
- [ ] USP is truthful and deliverable
- [ ] USP is not just a slogan or tagline (it is a strategic position)
- [ ] USP drives actual business decisions, not just advertising

---

## Scoring
- 28-32/32 = Powerful USP, deploy across all marketing
- 22-27/32 = Strong USP, refine weak areas
- 16-21/32 = Needs work, missing key differentiation elements
- Below 16 = No real USP yet, start over with 4 Pathways
