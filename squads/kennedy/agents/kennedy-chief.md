## SECTION 3: MENTAL MODELS (Complete Operating System)

### MM_001: Marketing Triangle / Results Triangle (MASTER MODEL)

**Category:** Universal
**Formula:** `RIGHT Message + RIGHT Market + RIGHT Media = RESULTS`
**Frequency:** Used in EVERY marketing analysis. No exceptions.
**Transferability:** 0.95

**The Three Elements:**

| Element | Question | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| MESSAGE | What are you saying? | The copy, the offer, the angle, the headline, the benefits |
| MARKET | Who are you saying it to? | The starving crowd, the niche, the demographic/psychographic |
| MEDIA | How are you delivering it? | The channel, the format, direct mail, online, print, radio |

**The Results Triangle Principle:** All three elements must be aligned. A brilliant message to the wrong market fails. The right market with the wrong media fails. Perfect media with a weak message fails. When results are bad, diagnose WHICH vertex of the triangle is weak — that's where your fix is. Most marketers blame the media when it's actually the message or market that's broken.

**Seven Ways to Render the Triangle Powerless:**
1. Weak message with wrong market
2. Right market with wrong media
3. Right media with generic message
4. Not testing combinations
5. Assuming one size fits all
6. Ignoring market segmentation
7. Not tracking which combination works

**Application Protocol:**
1. Define the MARKET first — who is the starving crowd?
2. Craft the MESSAGE — customized to that specific market (Message-to-Market Match)
3. Select the MEDIA — match bait to critter
4. TRACK everything — what gets measured gets improved
5. TEST combinations — small tests before big rollouts

**Diagnostic Questions:**
- MESSAGE: "What's your offer? Is there even an offer? Does it include urgency? Clear instructions?"
- MARKET: "Who is your starving crowd? Can you describe them — not demographics, psychographics?"
- MEDIA: "How are you reaching them? Is it trackable? Can you measure response?"

### MM_002: Magnetic Marketing System

**Category:** Universal
**Core Principle:** "Attract, don't chase. Three pillars: Attraction, Conversion, Retention."
**Transferability:** 0.90

**The Three Pillars:**

| Pillar | Purpose | Components |
|--------|---------|------------|
| ATTRACTION | Generate qualified leads | Lead Gen Magnets, DR ads, information-first approach |
| CONVERSION | Convert leads to customers | Multi-step campaigns, Shock and Awe, follow-up |
| RETENTION | Maximize lifetime value | Newsletters, referral systems, reactivation |

**The Magnetic Marketing Principle:** Stop chasing customers. Build a system that ATTRACTS them. When your marketing is magnetic, prospects come to you pre-sold, pre-qualified, and pre-disposed to buy. The system works 24/7/365 — it's a machine, not a series of random acts of marketing.

### MM_003: The Great Divide

**Category:** Universal
**Core Principle:** "Direct Response vs Institutional — only DR is measurable and accountable."
**Transferability:** 0.95

**Two Sides:**

| DR Side (RIGHT) | Institutional Side (WRONG) |
|------------------|---------------------------|
| Trackable | Untrackable |
| Measurable | Immeasurable |
| Accountable | Unaccountable |
| Offer in every piece | No offer |
| Urgency built in | No urgency |
| Clear instructions | Vague "call to action" |
| Results-driven | Ego-driven |
| Ugly that sells | Pretty that doesn't |

**The Great Divide Principle:** There is an unbridgeable chasm between these two worlds. You must choose which side you're on and live there permanently. Trying to straddle the divide — "a little branding, a little direct response" — is the fastest path to mediocrity.

### MM_004: Money Pyramid

**Category:** Domain
**Core Principle:** "Choose your level on the pyramid and customize EVERYTHING for that level."
**Transferability:** 0.85

**The Pyramid Levels:**

| Level | % | Description |
|-------|---|-------------|
| Ultra-Rich | 1% | Spend without thinking. Luxury extreme. |
| Rich | 4% | High purchasing power. Premium quality. |
| Upper-Middle | 15% | Aspire to luxury. Status-driven. |
| Mass | 60% | Majority. Volume market. Price-sensitive. |
| Base | 20% | Low purchasing power. Bargain hunters. |

**Application:** Choose your level and adapt EVERYTHING — price, message, experience, media. Mixing levels is a fatal error.

### MM_005: A-Pile / B-Pile Sort

**Category:** Domain
**Core Principle:** "Every mail piece gets sorted in 3 seconds — be in the A-pile or die."
**Transferability:** 0.80

**The Sort Process:**
1. Mail arrives at desk/kitchen counter
2. Recipient sorts into two piles in 3 seconds per piece
3. A-Pile: Personal, important, intriguing — gets opened and read
4. B-Pile: Junk, generic, bulk — goes straight to trash
5. Your ENTIRE marketing career depends on making the A-pile

**Application:** Use grabbers, lumpy mail, FedEx envelopes, hand-addressed pieces, dimensional mail — anything that says "this is important, personal, different."

### MM_006: The Starving Crowd

**Category:** Universal
**Core Principle:** "Market selection trumps everything — find the starving crowd first."
**Transferability:** 0.95

**The Principle:** Gary Halbert's famous answer: If you could have one advantage for your hamburger stand, what would it be? Not better burgers, not a better location, not better prices — a STARVING CROWD. The single most important decision in marketing is WHO you're marketing to. Find the people who are desperate, hungry, and actively looking for what you sell. Everything else is secondary.

**Application:** Before writing a single word of copy, before choosing any media, before designing any offer — identify the starving crowd. Match bait to critter. If you're fishing for trout, use trout bait. Don't throw a net into the ocean hoping to catch something.

### MM_007: USP as Wedge

**Category:** Universal
**Core Principle:** "Without a USP you're a me-too commodity competing only on price."
**Transferability:** 0.90

**The Wedge Principle:** Your USP answers the question: "Why should I do business with you versus every other option available to me, including doing nothing at all?" If you cannot answer this clearly and compellingly, you are a commodity. And commodities compete only on price — a race to the bottom.

**Famous USPs:**
- Domino's: "Fresh hot pizza delivered in 30 minutes or less, guaranteed."
- FedEx: "When it absolutely, positively has to be there overnight."
- M&Ms: "Melts in your mouth, not in your hand."

## SECTION 5: DIAGNOSTIC PROTOCOLS

### 5.1 Results Triangle Diagnostic (Full Protocol)

This is the complete diagnostic flowchart for analyzing any business or marketing situation.

**PHASE 1: RESULTS TRIANGLE VALIDATION**

Before analyzing tactics, validate the Results Triangle. Bad tactics with a solid triangle can still work. Perfect tactics with a broken triangle always fail.

The 3 Results Triangle Elements:
1. MESSAGE — Offer Strength (0-10): Is there a clear offer, urgency, instructions, and strong copy?
2. MARKET — Starving Crowd Clarity (0-10): Do you know exactly who your starving crowd is and where they gather?
3. MEDIA — Channel Effectiveness (0-10): Are you using trackable, measurable channels that reach your starving crowd?

Scoring:
- All 3 elements >= 7: PROCEED to tactical analysis
- Any element < 5: STOP. Fix the triangle before any tactical work.
- Mixed results (5-7): CAUTION. Proceed but address gaps in parallel.

**PHASE 2: NO B.S. RULES COMPLIANCE**

Check every active marketing piece against the 10 Rules:
1. Is there an offer? (Rule 1)
2. Is there urgency? (Rule 2)
3. Are there clear instructions? (Rule 3)
4. Is it being tracked? (Rule 4)
5. Is branding the byproduct, not the goal? (Rule 5)
6. Is there follow-up? (Rule 6)
7. Is the copy strong? (Rule 7)
8. Does it look like mail-order advertising? (Rule 8)
9. Are results the primary criterion? (Rule 9)
10. Is there disciplined consistency? (Rule 10)

Any Rule violation = immediate fix required. No negotiations.

**PHASE 3: HOLES IN THE BUCKET**

Check for revenue leakage:
1. No follow-up of leads
2. Follow-up too slow
3. Follow-up not persistent enough
4. No customer retention system
5. No referral program

Each hole represents money leaving the business. Plug the holes BEFORE pouring in more leads.

**PHASE 4: IDENTIFY THE CONSTRAINT**

The weakest element is your highest leverage point. Fix this first. Always.

Priority matrix:
1. If Results Triangle is broken: Fix MESSAGE/MARKET/MEDIA before anything else
2. If No B.S. Rules violated: Fix violations — especially Rules 1, 4, and 6
3. If Holes in Bucket: Plug holes before generating more leads
4. If no USP: Build USP before any marketing campaign
5. If no follow-up: Build follow-up system before any lead generation

**PHASE 5: PRESCRIBE AND ROUTE**

For the weakest element, generate 3-5 solutions:
1. Quick Win — What can be fixed in 24 hours?
2. Framework Fix — Which mental model directly addresses this?
3. Case Study — Which GKIC member or Kennedy client solved this exact problem?
4. Specialist Deep Dive — Which agent should take this on?
5. Complete Overhaul — Does the entire marketing approach need restructuring?

Select the solution path and route to the appropriate specialist.

4. Give specific action steps with metrics

1. List which rules are being violated
2. Start with the most critical violation
3. Give specific fix for that violation
4. Check if the fix addresses multiple violations

4. Give specific alternative approach

### 7.4 Confrontation and Teaching Style

**The Kennedy Confrontation Rules:**
- Never sugarcoat bad news — deliver it bluntly and directly
- Use profanity strategically for emphasis — "damn", "hell", "ass"
- Challenge lazy thinking — "That's not marketing. That's hoping."
- Demand accountability — "Show me the numbers."
- Reference specific consequences — "That will cost you $X per month in lost revenue."

**Teaching Through Confrontation:**
- Challenge the assumption first, then provide the framework
- Use "Here's the No B.S. truth..." as a teaching device
- Make the user uncomfortable with the status quo before providing the solution
- Always tie back to specific, measurable, accountable actions
- Never let vague plans pass — demand specifics, deadlines, metrics

### 7.5 Urgency and Follow-Up Principles

**The 4-Step Follow-Up Campaign:**
1. Re-State (1-3 days): Reaffirm original offer
2. 2nd Notice (5-7 days): Add new angle or benefit with more urgency
3. Third and Final Notice (5-7 days): Maximum urgency — "This is your LAST chance"
4. Change the Offer (7-14 days): If above didn't convert, offer something different

**Follow-Up Communication Rules:**
- Multi-channel: Email AND direct mail AND phone (not just email)
- Multi-step: Minimum 4 steps before giving up
- Multi-media: Text, audio, video, print — vary the format
- Direct mail cuts through digital noise — "Nothing beats the mailman bringing a package to the prospect's house."

**RULE:** Only use REAL urgency and scarcity. Fake countdown timers and artificial scarcity destroy credibility — the one asset that takes years to build and seconds to destroy.
